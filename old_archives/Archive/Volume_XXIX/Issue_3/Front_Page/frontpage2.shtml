Front Page
Author of Why the Left Hates America Speaks
by Charles Hallford
Staff Writer

Dan Flynn, author of Why the Left Hates America, talked to a packed audience.
Dan Flynn, author of the book Why the Left Hates America and editor of the newspaper 'Campus Report', spoke on November 11th in West Oak Lounge at Tressider Union about the greatness of the U.S. and the faults of anti-Americanism. Stanford College Republicans sponsored this event. Mr. Flynn began his discourse by explaining that "the left" in the title of his book, refers to "a very small portion of the population" that nonetheless has a "massive cultural influence." He said they have their "greatest impact on higher education." Mr. Flynn criticized liberal universities for promoting heterogeneity of ethnicity but not of thought. He said they have "diversity like a United Nations panel but think like a San Francisco coffeehouse."

Next, he described how leftist groups have reacted to both his speeches and his questions. He said he has been attacked more often at political events than in his career as a marine. One time, he went to a New Black Panther Reunion and asked them questions about their beliefs. Because they did not like his questions, they banned him for life from all future reunions. He also described his harassment when speaking at Cal where he was mooned and a student rushed onto the stage and tried to rip his microphone's cord out of the wall. After his speech there, the students led a Nazi-style book burning that included copies of Mr. Flynn's book.

Then Mr. Flynn showed a video clip performed by the comedic group Monty Python. The setting was 1st century Judea and a group of Judeans had gathered to badmouth Roman rule. The climax of the clip came when the leader of the group asked, "All right, but apart from the sanitation, the medicine, education, wine, public order, irrigation, roads, a fresh water system, and public health, what have the Romans ever done for us?"

Stanford Students, both conservative and liberal, gather in Tresidder Union’s West Oak Lounge to hear Dan Flynn, author of "Why the Left Hates America." America in practice rejects what the left teaches in theory, he says.
Mr. Flynn compared the American left to the leader in the clip, unreasonably brushing off all of America's contributions to the world. "What has America ever done for anybody?" Mr. Flynn asked. "How about technology?" Mr. Flynn pointed out that the U.S. leads the world in technology and listed numerous American inventions, such as the Internet and the light bulb.

He then went on to list even more contributions America has made to the world, the most important of which were freedom and self-government. "America stands for freedom throughout the world and always has," he argued. "America is not an imperial country." Mr. Flynn provided evidence for this argument with the fact that "the American ‘empire' [only] rules Americans."

If America has done so many great things for the world, then why does the left hate America? To answer this question, Mr. Flynn stated the thesis of his book, "What the left touts in theory, America refutes in practice." As an example, Mr. Flynn said the left claims that capitalism is a failed economic system. However, the U.S. with its capitalist free market is the richest country in the world.

"The left compares America to an ideal," Mr. Flynn said. He claimed that as a result the left only sees the negative aspects of the U.S. "You have to weigh the good and the bad," he said. Mr. Flynn stated that after doing so the logical conclusion is that the U.S. is the greatest country that has ever been. "Anti-Americanism is reflexive and knee-jerk."

After the conclusion of his speech, Mr. Flynn opened the floor for questions from the audience. "There were questions that were not, in nature, friendly," Travis Menk said after the event. Mr. Menk is the co-founder and co-president of Stanford College Republicans. "We knew [inimical questioning] was going to happen on the way in." While there was some definite hostility coming from some questioners, Mr. Flynn was neither physically nor verbally abused. "We're very proud that free speech prevails at Stanford still," Mr. Menk said.

During the question and answer segment, Mr. Flynn explained that "you have folks that hate this country because of religion." Furthermore, he said the left claims that Christianity, the predominant religion in this nation, is intolerant to other viewpoints. Mr. Flynn counters that by pointing out that in Christian countries people of all faiths are allowed to practice their beliefs while this is not the case in many non-Christian countries.

In addition, Mr. Flynn pointed out that even though he thinks highly of the U.S. he does not unilaterally agree with the Bush administration. "I don't know if governing Iraq would be best for us," he said. He then went on to explain why he does not think the U.S. should take down Saddam's regime. Even though Stanford College Republicans disagrees with Mr. Flynn on this point, members, after the event, expressed not anger but pleasure. They were glad to show outsiders that their speakers will not always promote every one of their viewpoints.

Later in the question and answer segment, Mr. Flynn was asked to reiterate his definition of "the left," which he did, aligning it with socialism and multiculturalism. "The vast majority of people in this country love America," he said, while the left sees the U.S. "as the embodiment of evil in the world." At the conclusion of the event, students were allowed to shake hands with Mr. Flynn and talk to him personally. Some fans even asked him to sign their copies of his book. When asked how he thought the event went, Mr. Menk said, "We were overly impressed with the turnout." Well over 100 students came to hear Mr. Flynn talk. Mr. Menk commented on the good representation amongst the audience of both the right and left sides of the political perspective. "It was a huge success."

At the event, copies of Campus Report and the Stanford Review were available at the door. Stanford College Republicans tried to have copies of Mr. Flynn's book available for purchase, but was unable to do so because of shipping problems. However, any students who still wish to purchase a copy can contact Mr. Menk at tmenk@stanford.edu, (650) 497-9108.
