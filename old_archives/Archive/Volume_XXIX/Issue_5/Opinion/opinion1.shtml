Opinion
A Call for a Palestinian CAMERA
by Aaron Masters
Contributing Writer

A Stanford student attending events sponsored by the Coalition for Justice in Israel / Palestine (CJIP), Students for Justice in Palestine (SJP), or the Stanford Israel Alliance (SIA) will hear a lot of accusations of media bias. The major problem is that only one of these groups - the SIA - can point to an organization that proves its point in a rigorous way. That pro-Israel news-monitoring organization is the Committee for Accurate Middle Eastern Reporting in America, or "CAMERA," www.camera.org.

CAMERA analysts track major U.S. media such as CNN, the New York Times, and National Public Radio, and point out omissions, distortions, and inaccuracies through CAMERA's own publications. CAMERA keeps some rein on its own editorializing, sticking generally to concrete media errors. It even has taken pains to protest inaccurate anti-Palestinian coverage such as the New York Times's mislabeling of a group of Israeli settler murderers as "vigilantes." All told, a regular consumer of news that keeps up with the CAMERA website can rest assured that she has seen a credible pro-Israel viewpoint.

But the pro-Palestinian viewpoint has no CAMERA. The closest organizations are Fairness in Accuracy and Reporting (www.fair.org) and Palestine Media Watch (www.pmwatch.org). Those pro-Palestinian sites lead the charge in decrying what they term pro-Israel media bias, but aren't as systematic or regular as their pro-Israel counterpart. And this is a problem for two big reasons. First, it leads to a lack of evidence for the oft-stated argument that the U.S. media is biased in favor of Israel, undermining the credibility of those fighting legitimately for the Palestinian cause. Second, this lack of a resource too often tempts those who feel emotionally or logically that there is a pro-Israel bias in the U.S. media to make their point through vile anti-Semitic accusations or odd coalitions that detract from their cause. Let me be specific on this latter point. More than once has a pro-Palestinian Stanford acquaintance emoted that with so much Jewish ownership or membership in the media, the Arabs / Palestinians / Muslims / non-Jewish activists are hopeless to expect fair coverage. That is of course a baseless and anti-Semitic assertion, though Jews can't fairly expect everyone making it to understand that. To see the falsehood and anti-Semitism there, one has to know the storied history of anti-Semitic conspiracy theoretic fabrications - lies claiming that Jews put "Jewish interests" above those of their profession or country - after which Jews were subsequently killed or deported. One also has to know that Jews have bitter and diverse debates on Israel and that the only people seriously trumpeting Jews in the media as a source of bias are hate group members and Holocaust deniers like David Irving. Ultimately, the bedfellows created by the "Jewish media bias" argument do more to delegitimize the plaintiff's case than to cast doubt on journalism.

Those without anti-Semitic interests - the majority of activists, I hope - need a pro-Palestinian CAMERA just as much. When journalist Robert Fisk levels his rather enormous charge that U.S. media has an incestuous relationship with a pro-Israel U.S. administration, some enormous data is needed. Unfortunately, Fisk's only evidence of pro-Israel bias or "overly official" coverage in his recent Stanford appearance was a piecemeal set of anecdotal examples from U.S. newspapers and a BBC ad that chose material unequally sympathetic to Israelis and Palestinians. This is not to say Fisk's conclusion is wrong. But when he goes on - with a similar lack of data - to suggest that pro-Israel lobbyists in the Bush administration could be the source of America's Near East policy and media problems, he risks attracting more fans from the "Jewish conspiracy" crowd than from intellectuals.

And again we see how the Palestinian CAMERA is so desperately needed. If U.S. media are using empty or overly official sources, misleading with language, or committing sins of omission in favor of Israel, we need a systematic analysis of just how, when, and where (and yes, "why" as Dr. Fisk would demand). While all who care about the Arab-Israeli conflict would benefit from such a resource, it is the legitimate Palestinian activists who need it most desperately. Only with it can they level bias charges with CAMERA-level rigor and eliminate the destructive temptations of hate.
