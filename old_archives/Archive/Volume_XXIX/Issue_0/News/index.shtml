A History of the Stanford Review
1987. Stanford University, under President Donald Kennedy, was at its most liberal. It was in that era that it abandoned the Western Civilization humanities program and replaced it with a politicized, anti-Western program. It was in that era when the University created a speech code to threaten anyone who spoke out in politically incorrect ways. And, in response, it was in that era that the Stanford Review was founded. ....Full story.....by Henry Towsner

Bush Finds Core Advisors at Hoover
Dateline: October 29, 1999 This article was written in the middle of Autumn Quarter, soon after Gov. Bush declared that he would run for President. In the years since, his connection to the Hoover fellows has only grown closer. Condoleeza Rice, formerly Stanford's Provost, is now President Bush's National Security Advisor. Others helped draft his daring policies on Social Security, education, and Medicare. Rumsfeld and Bush are working closely with Hoover on how to deal with terrorism and Iraq. In this article, the Review brought a story to campus--which later was picked up nationally--that most students were little aware of. ....Full story.....by Bob McGrew and Henry Towsner
