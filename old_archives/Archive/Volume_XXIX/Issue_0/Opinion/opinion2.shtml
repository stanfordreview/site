Opinion
What's the Theme of Theme Dorms?
Excerpts of an article
by John Gibbs

The big word these days is diversity - we are supposed to believe that more diversity is better for everyone. But what actually happens when we put an emphasis on diversity?

By "embracing" diversity, we have actually reduced diversity and supported separatism. We have separate dorm facilities for people of different races, separate campus offices which are supposed to be for a particular race, and many student groups which are for one particular race. Has anyone stopped to think that this is exactly against the original point? Wasn't the idea of the civil rights movement to bring people of different races together to get along despite their racial differences?

Suppose the original goal was to bring people of different races together.... If people of different races are separated, they obviously cannot interact since they live in different dorms, clearly negating this goal.

Suppose the original goal was to reduce racism between different ethnic and racial groups. If one's aim was to achieve this goal, it would seem that one would attempt to bring the different groups together to talk and interact. However, creating separate facilities for different groups does just the opposite: it separates the groups and reduces the chance for dialogue and interaction.

Suppose the original goal was to allow minority students to be around people of their own race. This goal in and of itself is not practical and Stanford should not support it. In the real world, students will not be able to be around students who are all their own race...Thus, if anything Stanford should be preparing those students for this reality by immersing them in a multi-racial environment, not isolating them in a single-race environment. In addition, this approach assumes that every minority student feels most comfortable around people who are his own race. This is by no means true.

Suppose the original goal of this separatism was to allow each group to be "represented" on campus. This idea is illogical. The notion that one office or campus group can represent every member of a particular race is appalling, as is the notion that each different race somehow has separate and distinct interests that other students don't have. It assumes that all members of a particular racial or ethnic group wish to define themselves by their race, and that they have no other interesting properties or interests by which to define themselves. Wasn't the whole point of the civil rights movement that people NOT define themselves based on race, but on their own unique personalities and interests?

It seems there is really no reason to have so many separate dorms and groups for each different race, so why do we have them? -- The people who support this kind of separatism are not held to any standard of accountability whatsoever. They do not have to show why and how their method of separatism has succeeded in reducing racism or bring students of different races together. Why? Any attempt to hold them accountable will probably be branded racist...not to mention highly "politically incorrect."
