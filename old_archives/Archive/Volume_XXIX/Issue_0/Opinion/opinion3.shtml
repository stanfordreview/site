Opinion
READ THIS SAVE $237
We've decided to save the children.

Despite a lifetime of unyielding allegiance to subverting any semblance of democracy, rationality, and happiness on the Farm, The Review has finally given in to its Maoist underbelly. Resistance is futile when the cause is this spirited. We want all students to know they can rise up against their oppressors and receive cash back from their university fees, thwarting the narrow-interest ASSU groups.

Here's what you can do: visit fees.stanford.edu. Click for the information about the fee refund. Type in the usual name and student ID. Click "yes" for all the buttons there. Click "submit" and then "yes." By the end of the quarter, you'll receive a crispy $76+ check. Do this for all three quarters and you save $237. Not bad for five minutes work of work, eh?

Each quarter, in addition to charging every student $12,000 for tuition, room, board, and chandeliers in building 30, Stanford assesses regular fees used to pay for the activities of and staff development expenses of ASSU groups. If you think that the ASSU can do a better job of distributing your money than you can, feel free to disregard this opportunity. Feel free also then to send the ASSU more of your own money. For $237 per student, you can guarantee that every student has his own special interest group. But if you're like us, save your money. Teach the ASSU-funded special interest groups to have the courtesy of showing up at your door and taking your money the old-fashioned way. True, weapons aren't allowed on campus, but we're sure they'll figure something out.
