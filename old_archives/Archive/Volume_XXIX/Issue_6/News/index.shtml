Friedman Speaks to Review Staff
Milton Friedman, world renowned economist and recipient of the 1976 Nobel Prize for economic science, graced Stanford students with his presence at an open lunch meeting on Tuesday, February 4th. Born in the United States in 1912, he is perhaps most distinguished for his book Capitalism and Freedom, in which he presents his view of an ideal economic system founded upon the principle of private enterprise operating in a free market. ....Full story.....by Silvia Cruz

UCLA Bruin Republicans Hold Affirmative Action Bake Sale, Angering Righteous Liberals
In an effort to educate UCLA students on hypocrisy of affirmative action, an issue currently on the Supreme Court docket with the Michigan case, the UCLA Bruin Republicans held an Affirmative Action Bake Sale at the bottom of Bruin Walk, Monday, February 3, 2003. The Bruin Republicans sold cookies from Diddy Riese, a Westwood favorite, under the following price structure:
Black, Hispanic and American Indian Females: 25 cents
Black, Hispanic and American Indian Males: 50 cents
White Females: $1.00
White Males and All Asian-Americans: $2.00 ....Full story
