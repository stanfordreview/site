Opinion
Conservative Argument Against War
by Jeffrey Lonsdale
Contributing Writer

Right now, it's hard to be someone who is for limited government and at the same time against the war on Iraq. I find myself agreeing with people I never agree with, and disagreeing with people who are usually on my side on most issues. Too many people assume that if you are for minimal government, you also have to be for the war. On the surface, this doesn't make much sense. It seems to follow naturally that someone who is for tax cuts and cuts in government spending would not want our government to go spend more money on a war. After all, war is the health of the state. If you want to cut back government, going to war is not the way to do it. War grows the government. When government grows, freedom most certainly does not.

Before we go into Iraq, we really need to think about what we are getting into. The war itself has been estimated to cost us around $200 billion. When the war is over, there will be more money spent covering the cost of occupying Iraq. Now, this sounds a lot like the beginning of a leftist argument: "we need to spend the money on social programs at home rather than wasting money in a war". That isn't the point though; we need to spend less money, period. This war is another example of the US wasting taxpayer money on useless programs, like the Department of Agriculture, the Department of Education, and other failed government endeavors much too numerous to list. Now, national security is one thing the federal government should actually bother concerning itself with. However, even if Iraq is developing weapons of mass destruction, it doesn't matter. Deterrence works. Saddam won't attack us because he doesn't want to lose his state or his life. Someone who is so paranoid that he sleeps in a different palace every night wouldn't do something that would be sure to end his reign and his life. Deterrence has worked in the past, and it will work in the future.

Our government's job is to keep us safe. It should do so by following the constitution and the principles this country was founded on. The President shouldn't have the power to go to war when he feels like it. The founders specifically gave this power to Congress. The Congress shouldn't be able to give up that power to the President. Antiwar leftists who only appeal to the constitution when it supports their antiwar view and neocons who only talk about the constitution when they are trying to convince liberals that the federal government shouldn't waste money on a domestic program are both inconsistent. Our constitution should be followed to the letter in both foreign and domestic matters. Our federal government is only supposed to protect its citizens. It shouldn't be getting involved in the rest of the world. One of the main things it should do is avoid unnecessarily angering other countries. We don't know that the terrorists hate the US for our freedoms. They could just as easily hate the US because of our government's foreign policy. Where is the justification in the constitution for what our government is doing? It simply isn't there.

People say that taking Saddam Hussein out of power is a good thing, as it is removing an evil state from the world, so the war should be supported. Following this logic, our government could try to do many good things. We could take out all of the evil dictators of the world, feed the poor everywhere, make sure a 'good' state never loses in a major conflict around the world. . . . there really isn't a limit to the good things we could try to do to help. If a group of people tried to privately raise money to achieve these goals I would applaud them. However, it isn't the responsibility of the United States government to do this. Our government was created to protect the rights of our own citizens, not the world. Just as humans are born free into society with no obligation to other people, our government does not have any obligation to the other states and people of the world. It shouldn't be taking money from its own citizens to try to help those of other countries. We should let the other industrialized countries of the world take care of their own problems.

Government action at home follows the same principles as government action abroad. If there appears to be a problem, government may seem like a quick fix, but it isn't the answer. It only leads to other unforeseen consequences. Conservatives who support minimal government should think long and hard before supporting a war on Iraq.
