Stanford Peace Group, Greens, Muslims Rally Against Congress War Resolution
On Thursday, October 3rd, 2002, a coalition of several Stanford groups led by the Stanford Coalition for Peace and Justice (SCPJ) held a "Words Against War" rally in protest about the House and Senatorial votes scheduled to be held later that month on resolution for war in Iraq. The other organizations sponsoring the rally included the Stanford Green Party and the Muslim Student Awareness Network (MSAN). Students were supplied with cell phones and phones numbers for the White House and California senators and House representatives. Although they were told that they could say anything they wished, would-be callers were also supplied with scripts to read criticizing the Bush administration's policies on issues ranging from the upcoming Congressional vote regarding the war in Iraq to Bush's request for a strong U.N. resolution against Iraq. Students who participated in the call-in had their votes tabulated and the overall results were apparently recorded for posterity. ....Full story in News.....by Harrison Osaki

FBI's Mueller speaks on Liberty, Security
FBI Director Robert. S. Mueller III was on campus Friday to receive the Jackson H. Ralston award from the Stanford Law School. Accompanied by security and principles, Mr. Mueller addressed an audience of alumni and students with his simple message of balancing personal freedoms and the interests of national security. Speaking in a thoughtful, respectful way, Mr. Mueller championed the debate over these topics as the "critical discourse in this country". ....Full story in News.....by William E. Hudson

Opera Comes to Stanford
On October 5th, Lively Arts brought the San Francisco Opera to Stanford with Western Opera Theater's production of the perennial favorite: Puccini's La Boheme. While this may not be the highlight of the Lively Arts season (see accompanying article for more information), it was certainly a sound competitor. I was expecting a lot from La Boheme. I expected to hear some gorgeous singing: rich baritone runs, glass-breaking soprano trills, and stirring love arias -- I did. I expected to laugh, cry, and gasp in shock -- I did. I expected to leave saying to myself "Puccini would have been proud" -- I did. . . .but in a slightly different order: "would Puccini have been proud?" ....Full story in Entertainment.....by Ming Zhu

Democrats Should Support an Attack
The Democratic Party has had a long history of political ambivalence and indecision when it comes to matters of war. In 1991, we warned of how the world's oil markets would collapse, thousands of Americans would die, and how Iraq could quickly become the Vietnam of the Middle East. When it came time to vote, a large majority of Democrats voted against authorizing force to backup the UN resolution that would liberate Kuwait. The war was not a Vietnam; it was not even a Korea. After committing troops to the ground it took less than 100 hours to liberate Kuwait and crush what remained of Iraq's once feared army. This political victory gave back 10 house seats to the Republicans in 1992 and left many Democrats looking overly paranoid and 30 years in the past. It is time for us to stand up as Democrats and embrace a war resolution rather than fear it. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Stephen Cohen
