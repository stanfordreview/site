A Word From The Editor
Editor's Note
"I love oil more than Iraqis."

No, not really. Despite my dark and devilish appearance in this picture, like the enthusiastic revelers at the peace rally, I, too, "love Iraqis more than oil." In fact, I think it would be nice to rid Iraq of its insane dictator and bring greater freedom to that area, even if it would mean losing their oil. After all, I enjoy the inexpensive acceleration provided by my purely hydrocarbon-powered automobile engine, but innocent Iraqis not dying to nerve gas and Israelis not getting incinerated in possible nuclear explosions is even more desirable than quickly getting up to speed on the 101.

But that's missing the point. There is something about this standard peace-rally rhetoric that really bothers me. It should bother you too, regardless of who you are. Their assumption is that if (a) you are doing something that benefits yourself, or (b) you believe in any foreign policy that involves the use of force, then you are EVIL and STUPID. You horrible misanthropic naive bigoted white man.

The conservative argument on Iraq is that the danger of continued inaction with regard to Saddam Hussein outweighs the "troubles" imposed by action; additionally, it is morally sound to remove defiant, violent, evil mass-murderers against whom you've had to fight wars, from positions of power. The intellectuals on the left, such as those at our Center for International Security and Cooperation (CISAC), argue that the instability introduced by taking action in Iraq may not be justified due to risks inherent in imposing a regime change in a highly volatile region, and the lack of a suitable plan for Iraq's future.

Good, that's productive, and those are the details that need to be discussed. While I favor an immediate forced regime change for various reasons, I can respectfully disagree with someone who seriously believes, for example, that action at this time may cause a coup in Pakistan to put nuclear weapons into the hands of Islamic extremists, leaving us worse off than before.

I cannot, however, respect someone who tells me that unlike myself, they are not "in a hurry to bomb someone, anyone". They "care about human life", unlike those who disagree with them. They Ôknow' that "America only wants money and power, and will make up any lie to attack someone with oil...because [we] enjoy killing those [we] see as inferior." Clearly, when conservatives advocate attacking Iraq, they aren't advocating people dying -- they're advocating taking action to save lives. When Truman, a liberal, dropped the bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki, he saved lives. I am not saying that everyone should necessarily agree with this solution, but if you cannot see the possible connection between the use of force and saving lives, there is something very, very wrong with your ability to reason.

It's interesting how most popular political discussion takes place at such a low intellectual level, even here at The Farm. We build fences around our minds as our tribal instincts seek to place boundaries and define our positions. The standard party line on both sides is easy to grasp, but it seems that most of the time, both sides possess a distorted and "how are you so stupid" view of the other's opinions.

For example, most liberals will tell you that Bill Simon will try to ban abortion if given the chance, and is thus a nightmare. (As you read this, he has no doubt lost the election to that horrible, horrible man about whom I will not rant at this time.) As Alec Rawls notes in one part of his article on Republicanism, the only legislatively relevant abortion view on which most California Republicans disagree with the Dem's is that the government should not be paying for abortions, because everybody doesn't necessarily agree with it, and people should not be forced to give money to something they find morally abhorrent.

Similarly, some Republicans, looking to the extremists in the Democratic party and the rate at which they are growing the government, think that Democrats must want a society where everyone is a homogenously lower-middle class government employee. Everyone would use public transportation, have multiple sexual partners, be forced to wait in long lines for healthcare, goods and services, and own nothing that all his neighbors do not also own. (Because that would destroy a sense of community). Talented businessmen would compete to teach in public schools, because it would pay better. Entrepreneurialship, creating value through capitalism, and hiring people for an agreed-upon amount of money would be seen for the evil exploitation it is.

As the poor and "humble" masses realized that democracy through the Democrats allowed them to have whatever their betters had earned for themselves, forced sharing of expensive exercise club memberships and a distribution of "psychologically beneficial food" from expensive restaurants would go into place. After all, it's not fair that only the wealthy get to have lobster. Especially if they're white. Who knows what else would follow, as we tread down the path of calling the ability to steal from others a "right". This is what many Republicans think of Democrats.

And, while its true that many Democrats from Norway, for example, apparently enjoy this type of society, Democrats are generally not socialists. They just don't realize that socialism (EVIL) is what all their well-intentioned policies will lead to, is all. So be nice to them.

We have some great reporting and opinions pieces for you this issue. Alex Robbins talks about one issue the liberal extremists got right, if for the wrong reasons -- Airport Screeners. Alec Rawls is amazing as usual with his explanation of the Ideal of Liberty versus the Ideal of Democracy. Also, check out Nels on Snipers, and Will on misguided liberalism.
