Stanford College Republicans Lead Effort to Return ROTC to Campus
The Stanford College Republicans have launched their first major initiative: an attempt to bring the military's Reserve Officer Training Corps back to campus. ROTC has been gone from the Stanford campus since 1969, the height of the Stanford anti-war movement, and the sporadic efforts to bring it back to campus have so far been an unmitigated failure. ....Full story in Front Page.....by Bob McGrew and Charles Hallford

ASSU Vice-President Pushes New Student Union
For many new students, the Tresidder Memorial Union is one of the first sites they see on campus. Tresidder hosts a variety of student services and stores such as a Jamba Juice, TresEx (a small shopping store) a computer cluster, a pair of banks and the home offices of the ASSU and the OSA. Recently, however, a new student-led movement has risen pressing for the development of a New Student Union (NSU) to either supplement or replace the existing Tresidder facilities and building. ....Full story in Front Page.....by Harrison Osaki

Editor's Note
"I love oil more than Iraqis."No, not really. Despite my dark and devilish appearance in this picture, like the enthusiastic revelers at the peace rally, I, too, "love Iraqis more than oil." In fact, I think it would be nice to rid Iraq of its insane dictator and bring greater freedom to that area, even if it would mean losing their oil. After all, I enjoy the inexpensive acceleration provided by my purely hydrocarbon-powered automobile engine, but innocent Iraqis not dying to nerve gas and Israelis not getting incinerated in possible nuclear explosions is even more desirable than quickly getting up to speed on the 101.....Full story in A Word From The Editor.....by Chris Fish

SCPJ Protests Citizenship Requisite, Federalization of Airport Workers
Several campus activist organizations, led by Stanford Community for Peace and Justice (SCPJ), the have led a campaign against the proposal that all US Customs workers must be US citizens. This proposal's reasoning presumably is that non-citizens are unsuitable for these positions due to the degree of trustworthiness and loyalty required. The students of SCPJ vehemently disagree with this idea, apparently believing that there is no unique loyalty from US citizens to their country. ....Full story in News.....by Nels Hansen

Left Extremists are Right on Airport Screeners
A recent Daily article on the San Francisco peace protests quoted a number of students as voicing the opinion that the protests were too omnibus, too chalk-full of leftist rhetoric to make more moderate anti-war individuals feel comfortable. In the last week I also read an anti-war article from a libertarian point of view that mentioned the same issue: just because one opposes a war on Iraq does not necessarily mean that one also hates globalization or wants to free Mumia. This tendency for a movement's leadership to come from the extremes often leads to the somewhat absurd situation of the political margin effectively marginalizing the center. In marginalizing the center, however, activists can sometimes become their own worst enemies. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Alex Robbins

The Snipers Lived in My Community
By now you have all heard of this (or these?) sniper(s) that murdered many innocent Americans on the East Coast as they went about their daily lives. Turns out, these fellows spent a great deal of time in Bellingham, where I am from. Not only that, they hung out in the same local coffeehouse that I did. Played chess there just as I did. And in general were like many local transients; hanging out in one of the few quasi-public spaces that was open until midnight. Stuart's Coffee had faux-everything. We had psuedo-intellectuals, people trying to act like artists to impress their peers, people playing at paganism and various forms of 'ancient' spirituality which had been 'rediscovered' recently, and lots of people who just liked being in a weird social setting. In a sense, a freak show with extremely expensive milkshakes. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Nens Hansen

Republicans, Democrats and Republicanism
Our republican form of government, according to the political philosophy of the Adamses, is the system of liberty under law. The securing of liberty requires, first of all, that the people be free to choose who shall govern them, so that they can throw off any would be tyrant. Thus democracy is not the founding principle of our government. It is a means to the founding principle of liberty. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Alec Rawls
