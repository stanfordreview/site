Week in Brief
University Cracks Down on Alcohol?
Last Tuesday, Dean of Students Marc Wais told the ASSU Senate that the recent spate of alcohol probation by the Office of Student Activities does not constitute a crackdown by the University. Fraternities Sigma Chi, Kappa Sigma, and Theta Delta Chi, and the Stanford Marching Band are currently on alcohol probation. The OSA's policy of probation and punishments range from community service to loss of housing. The administration plans to distribute a survey of alcohol use on campus. Associate Dean of Students Marcus Graves blames the manner in which large parties have been conducted in past, and saying that organizers should not admit students who are clearly drunk, dissuade minors from bringing alcohol, and check IDs and assign wristbands at the door. Graves warned that RAs and RFs in frosh dorms might have to enforce a prohibition in the event of an alcohol-related death.

Online Forum Discusses Iraq
Stanford, Oxford, and Yale joined together in Alliance for Lifelong Learning's new online forum, in which 6 professors presented their arguments supporting or discouraging war in Iraq. Over 5,000 people registered for the 4 day discussion that began Tuesday, October 29. Each professor had 15 to 30 minutes of streaming-video time to give their perspective, followed by a period for questions and answers. Three Stanford instructors participated: Thomas Sheehan, professor of religious studies, as well as Joel Beinin and Ahmad Dallal, professors of history.

Sports at Stanford
With the exception of a few rough spots, Stanford athletic teams excelled this week. Although Varsity football's woes deepened--the team (2-5, 1-3) posted a 28-18 loss to UCLA on October 26--crew, golf, tennis, and water polo had strong showings. Women's tennis is sending freshman Alice Barnes and the doubles team of junior Lauren Barnikow and sophomore Erin Burdette to the Intercollegiate Tennis Association's National Indoor Tennis Championship on November 8. In water polo, 2001 NCAA Player of the Year Tony Azevedo posted a total of eight goals for Stanford in victories over UC Irvine and UCLA, earning Stanford Athletics' "Cardinal of the Week" title. On Saturday, November 2, Stanford football attempts to right its luck at No. 16 Oregon (6-2, 2-2), and ninth-ranked men's soccer looks to avenge last Saturday's 1-0 loss at No. 8 Cal in a rematch at home.
