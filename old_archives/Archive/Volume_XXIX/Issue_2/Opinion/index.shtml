Left Extremists are Right on Airport Screeners
A recent Daily article on the San Francisco peace protests quoted a number of students as voicing the opinion that the protests were too omnibus, too chalk-full of leftist rhetoric to make more moderate anti-war individuals feel comfortable. In the last week I also read an anti-war article from a libertarian point of view that mentioned the same issue: just because one opposes a war on Iraq does not necessarily mean that one also hates globalization or wants to free Mumia. This tendency for a movement's leadership to come from the extremes often leads to the somewhat absurd situation of the political margin effectively marginalizing the center. In marginalizing the center, however, activists can sometimes become their own worst enemies. ....Full story.....by Alex Robbins

The Snipers Lived in My Community
By now you have all heard of this (or these?) sniper(s) that murdered many innocent Americans on the East Coast as they went about their daily lives. Turns out, these fellows spent a great deal of time in Bellingham, where I am from. Not only that, they hung out in the same local coffeehouse that I did. Played chess there just as I did. And in general were like many local transients; hanging out in one of the few quasi-public spaces that was open until midnight. Stuart's Coffee had faux-everything. We had psuedo-intellectuals, people trying to act like artists to impress their peers, people playing at paganism and various forms of 'ancient' spirituality which had been 'rediscovered' recently, and lots of people who just liked being in a weird social setting. In a sense, a freak show with extremely expensive milkshakes. ....Full story.....by Nens Hansen

Republicans, Democrats and Republicanism
Our republican form of government, according to the political philosophy of the Adamses, is the system of liberty under law. The securing of liberty requires, first of all, that the people be free to choose who shall govern them, so that they can throw off any would be tyrant. Thus democracy is not the founding principle of our government. It is a means to the founding principle of liberty. ....Full story.....by Alec Rawls
