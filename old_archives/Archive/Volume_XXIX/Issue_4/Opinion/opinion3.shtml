Opinion
Heterosexual affect is now illegal in California
by Alec Rawls
Senior Staff Writer

Life played a cruel trick on Eddie Araujo. It gave him a girl's brain in a boy's body. He spent the last years of his young life proclaiming those crossed wires. He dressed and acted like the girl who in his mind he was. He made himself as pretty as he could and went out to attract boys. When three young men at an east bay party found out that Eddie was physically male theybecame irate and killed him.

Fraud and murder are two wrongs. Now California is making it three by charging the killers with a hate crimes enhancement. Araujo's fraud was a provocation. According to established principles of law, that is a mitigating circumstance, not an aggravating circumstance. To interpret it as an aggravating circumstance is to do exactly what the law cannot do in a free society: give the reasonable affective desires and reactions of some people legal priority over the reasonable affective desires and responses of others.

Homosexual activism is full of plaintive appeals that heterosexuals should feel flattered when homosexuals express attraction to them. Obviously, from an objective point of view, attraction is a compliment. How unfair not to take it as such! How unfair that making passes at young heterosexual men is a good way for a homosexual to get his head crammed down a sewer.

Granted, this IS unfair. But objectivity has nothing to do with it. Heterosexuals are innately configured to find homosexuality repulsive, just as most people innately revolt at certain smells. Making a homosexual advance towards a heterosexual is like serving him a plate of stink. It is NOT going to be felt as a complement.

This is not to say that there are no elements of malleability to human sexuality, but all of us know from introspection that sexuality is largely and firstly innate. Witness Eddie Araujo. Eddie, or Lida as he called himself, understood his mind to be unalterably female.

Embracing this sexual identity, he rejected that he could not act like other girls who went to parties and tried to attract boys. In effect, he pursued homosexual advances towards heterosexuals from the female role. The kid was dealt a tough hand and he lived it as bravely as he could. Good for him. Such behavior is not necessarily protected by the full ideal of liberty, which is based on the proposition that indirect or vicarious interests in what other people think or do cannot justify harm to anyone's direct interests: their life of liberty. Eddie's case falls into that gray area where the interests on both sides are indirect. His interest in dressing pretty is an interest in what other people think, and their revulsion (those who are revolted) is a vicarious reaction, not a reaction to direct harm. With no direct interests on either side, liberty is not a matter of right.

It is also not a matter of wrong. There are no laws against cross-dressing, and it was not necessarily unreasonable for Araujo to assert his interests. Where interests were in conflict, he chose not to take all the burden on himself. To be true to himself, he put some of the burden on others, committing a very personal kind of fraud that gave others reason to be angry.

Three young men appear to have reacted to that reasonable anger in unreasonable fashion. If they are guilty of murder, they will pay for it for the rest of their lives. But they are certainly not guilty of MORE than murder. To charge a hate crime enhancement is to say that their anger at being defrauded by Araujo was itself a crime: a thought crime.

A basic principle of liberty is that we punish people for their actions, not their thoughts. What matters is not what attracts or repels you, but how you act on what you feel. We will tolerate what we can, and what we CAN tolerate is limited by what people do, not what they think. The criminalization of thought is a rejection of tolerance and liberty.

The Araujo case shows how far this has already been taken. Normal heterosexual response is now officially illegal in California. The homosexual fantasy that heterosexuals should react positively to their advances now carries the force of law. Overreact negatively to such an advance and your normal revulsion to homosexuality will in itself be charged as a ADDITIONAL crime.

This is a step into totalitarianism: a step away from tolerance and into forced approval. How astounding that homosexuals, of all people, would be the ones demanding that the wrong affective response should in itself be considered a crime. Depending on tolerance, they refuse to reciprocate. Now THAT is perverse.
