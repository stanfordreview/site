A Word from the Editor
Editor's Note
by Chris Fish
Editor-in-Chief

"Peace Activists."

Sure, they usually don't have too solid a grasp on the reality of international relations, but what harm can they do with their beautiful beliefs?

There is a song we sometimes sing in Hebrew at Shabbat Services, and the words translate to "Nation shall not rise up against nation; they shall study war no more." (Isaiah 2:4). It sounds so simple and wonderful, and I am often tempted to retreat into a happy shell, and embrace the simple philosophy of never calling for war. God could clearly not desire so much hate and violence as now exists. If America embraced a nonviolent policy, wouldn't itmake the world a better place?

No.

No, it would not. It would destroy most peace the world currently enjoys.

If everyone in the world embraced this philosophy of peace at all costs, it would work. But if only 99.99 percent did, the philosophy would still fail.

We must view the world from a realistic perspective, one that recognizes that corruption and evil men exist. Saddam Hussein, for instance, is a murdering, duplicitous supporter of terrorism who employs a professional rapist to humiliate men and their wives in front of their families, lest they think of disagreeing with him. Anyone with decency and a shred of humanity should hate him with a passion. There are lots of powerful evil men like this in the world.

The reason you need a government in the first place is to prevent other people from abridging your freedoms. Lacking morality, force is the only tool evil understands. If you get rid of this force entirely, whichever of several evil groups happen to have the most firepower will take control and start pillaging and raping innocent communities. Having force is not always enough. When evil rears its head and presents a threat to the innocent, the force must be promptly displayed. If you are unable to show your power and ability to counter evil, more of it will arise. The price of liberty is constant vigilance.

Without the United States standing up for freedom, much more of the world would be suffering under corrupt regimes, and Communism and Socialism. Without a force for good willing to shine through the dark, what peace we have would make our reality seem a calm sunny day relative to the fierce storms of fighting and corruption that would be roaring across the world. By supporting the United States' and the Bush administration‘s policies of using force when its use is necessary to protect ourselves against direct and indirect threats, you are promoting peace better than anyone who has retreated under the unthinking shell of "peace activism at all costs."

By going and yelling about the need for peace, these activists are often promoting policies that indirectly cause more violence to arise in the world.

And incidentally, the other harm these "peace activists" can cause is more direct. Jane Fonda toured Vietnam as an activist and was often brought to see POW's, to see how "well" they were being treated. These men were living in horrid conditions, sometimes at 60 percent their healthy body weight, and were mistreated and beaten often. For activists like her, they were cleaned up, shaved, given decent clothing, and paraded in a line. One group, given time to prepare, made tiny pieces of paper with their SSN's so that their wives and families would know they were alive after three years. As she went down the line shaking hands with each one saying things like, "Don't you feel bad for bombing babies?", they each slipped her their piece of paper, thinking her silly criticisms were surely an act.

When Ms. Fonda got to the end, to their disbelief, she took all the little papers she had gathered and handed them to the Viet Cong commander. Three of the men died from the beatings they received for this stunt. Despite actions like this, the far-left reveres activists like Ms. Fonda. If it was up to me, she would have to go live in poverty in a Communist country, and be put through what those men endured if she then resisted. So no, peace activism is not always harmless. If we used it as a policy, it would cause great violence and harm to rack the world. And when people like Ms. Fonda exist and do what she did, great harm is also done. It is my love of peace and justice that makes me despise peace activist groups like "Peace and Justice". The road to hell is paved with good intentions.

There is another issue I'd like to address that is very controversial. I question Stanford's Muslim community for its silence with regard to the great evil being commited in its religion's name throughout the world. It is very disturbing what we are hearing from the Stanford Islamic activists.

I realize that Islam is not a religion of violence and terrorism; however, if the religion is indeed being "hijacked", why are you not screaming bloody murder at the injustices being done in your name? Why are so few of you fighting on our side?

The only loud voices I hear in the Muslim Community are those crying out in fear of possible racism at airports, or at the "evil of Israel." I see nobody complaining at the rampaging anti-semitism in the Muslim press. I see nobody standing up and screaming that suicide bombing is wrong, and that Allah does not support these evil countries who give money to terrorists and twist Islam into an evil religion.

If the religion is being twisted, say something about it. Hold a rally against all this ACTUAL EVIL. It would utterly sicken me if someone went and killed a bunch of innocent people at a hotel or a business and then said that God approved, and that he was happy the children and innocents had died. I would call him and anybody who associated with him evil, and help those fighting him.

Why, I want to know, is the Muslim community here at Stanford so vocal in anti-Israel groups, but so quiet when it comes to complaining about the terror racking the world in the name of Islam?

If you truly think your religion is being misused, it is your job to stand up and make it abundantly clear which side you stand on. Because I and a lot of others are very disturbed to hear about widespread attacks in the name of Allah, then hear only anti-American and anti-Israel rhetoric from our Muslim friends here on campus.
