Stanford Student Enterprises to Provide Financial Information
Last month, Stanford Student Enterprises (SSE) decided to release its financial documents to The Stanford Review and The Stanford Daily after several months of stonewalling. The decision to disclose the information came after former Review editor Henry Towsner and former Daily editor Mark Boucher brought the issue before the ASSU's Constitutional Council, representing their respective publications. SSE will be releasing information including income data and balance statements for the ASSU and SSE organizations to the public; separate, proprietary information such as information about SSE's specific business units, will be released exclusively to the Daily and Review under a non-disclosure agreement. The initial information request by the Daily and Review to the SSE was made on April 24th, 2002, when the Review and the Daily jointly sent a letter to the President of SSE Ross Davisson asking for "access to the salaries of all SSE employees, a copy of SSE's financial statements from the past three years, and a listing of SSE's current investments". Despite Mr. Towsner's efforts to negotiate with Mr. Davisson (which resulted in an agreement to limit the information request to SSE balance sheets, SSE student employee salaries and current investments), Mr. Davisson eventually rejected the request, although he did say that he would provide a listing of investments. ....Full story.....by Harrison Osaki
