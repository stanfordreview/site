SLAC Encounters Difficulties

The Stanford Labor Action Coalition (SLAC) held a town hall style panel to try to raise awareness about their campaign for all workers on campus to receive a “living wage.” The panel consisted of SLAC member Matt Seriff-Cullick, United Stanford Workers President Zev Kvitky, Stanford Gardener Jose Escanuela, and ABM Janitor Doroteo Garcia. It attracted an audience of about 20 people at the El Centro Chicano lounge on November 29th. Stanford President John Hennessey and Executive Director of Human Resources Diane Peck were invited on short notice but were unable to attend.

 

The Problem with Stanford’s Introduction to the Humanities

“The problem with IHUM is a manifestation of the problem of students’ inability to grasp that there is no problem.” Makes sense, right? No, that was not a quote from an IHUM fellow, though it sounds like it could have been. After all, IHUM fellows tend to address students’ concerns in a similarly vague, dismissive manner: the problem is with the students, not with the material, teaching method, or ideology. To an extent, this is true. Freshman students do not enter IHUM adequately prepared. Even students who easily score 5s on the English AP find their IHUM classes unreasonably challenging. But what makes IHUM so unreasonable?

Island Politics

For the second time in six years, a coup of Fiji government occurred. Commodore Voreqe Bainimarama, head of Fiji’s military, led the overthrow of the new democratically elected government. On the night of December 4th, the military disarmed the police force and placed government officials under house arrest. He demanded that the government, led by Prime Minister Laisenia Qarase, stop attempts to pass legislation that would grant amnesty to individuals involved in the 2000 coup, along with other legislation that he sees as favoring the native Fijians over the Indian minority. He also wanted to see the removal of the Police Commission from office since he was Australian.

An Interview with Dr. John Koza

Dr. John R. Koza, a Consulting Professor in the Department of Medicine and Electrical Engineering at Stanford, recently took time to talk with The Review about the progress of his proposal for electoral system reform in the United States. In 2006, Professor Koza, a lifelong student of the United States electoral system, co-authored a 620 page book on the subject entitled Every Vote Equal: A State-Based Plan For Electing the President By National Popular Vote. He is also considered the inventor of genetic programming, for which he has received much acclaim, and co-invented the scratch-off instant lottery game ticket used by state lotteries.

The Tamil Tigers and the Battle for Sri Lanka

The twenty-three year civil war between the government of Sri Lanka and the Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam (LTTE) continues to escalate. While the majority of Sri Lanka’s population is ethnically Sinhalese, the minority ethnic group, the Tamils, make-up the majority of the north and east of the island. The Sinhalese are mostly Buddhists that speak Sinhala; the Tamils are linked by language, but their ethnic composition is a mix of Hindu, Christian, and Muslim.
