The Debate Continues

In 1996, the voters of California sent shockwaves of race-neutrality through the country by passing Proposition 209. Much to the chagrin of left-wing advocacy groups, the passage meant the end of discrimination on the basis of sex, race and nationality in California businesses and universities. What a novel idea -- that in America, the government might judge citizens on merit rather than a rigid race box! Of course, the ivory tower of academia didn’t receive the news too well. Their solution? Declare a “diversity crisis.” As our friends at the Cal Patriot report, Chancellor Birgeneau has decided to make circumventing democracy his first priority in his new position and has issued a “call to action” against Proposition 209. Cutting right to the point, Birgeneau stated on a recent radio interview, “I think we need to be able to take [race] into account.” To this end, Berkeley is launching a new think tank. The Earl Warren Institute on Race, Ethnicity and Diversity seeks to define the “the legal limits on aggressive efforts to promote inclusion in higher education” and an “agenda to inform future public consideration of revisions or repeal” of Prop. 209. In short, the taxpayers of California are now funding an effort to overturn their own will. We’ll keep you updated on how Chancellor Birgeneau’s term develops throughout this volume.
