Beyond Stanford University: Part 3

by Navin Kadaba

Features Editor

 

Forward Article to a Friend

Print Article

 

At this point, you all are probably pretty well settled here on campus. Your dorm rooms are stocked, you have a solid eating schedule, and classes are getting in rhythm. Now that these things are all figured out, you naturally begin to think: what do I do with my free time? (And yes, there is such a thing). Sure, you can spend your weekend nights working like all the other nights, or going to a party, but after a while the routine can get monotonous. As a result, we must turn again to opportunities off campus. A car isn’t always necessary (but it is often helpful) when making an off-campus excursion.

 

Movies

Flicks is nice for movies that are somewhat new, but when a Blockbuster hits the theatre, who wants to wait until next quarter?

 

Century 16

Mountain View

Century 16 is a standard theatre, playing most new hit movies. They offer a student price during the weekdays, and matinee prices on weekend morning/early afternoons. They also have alternate row seating, where one row with bright red seats reclines, and the other row with deep cardinal seats have retractable cup holders for when you go to a movie with that special someone.

 

AMC 20

Santa Clara

AMC is a bit farther than Century, but the theatre is of a higher quality that might be worth that trek depending on the person.

 

Metreon

San Francisco

The farthest away but by far the best theatre. It plays most new hits, along with a sprawling IMAX theatre that plays the IMAX version of some of the best movies of the year (e.g. Batman Begins, the upcoming Harry Potter). They also offer stadium seating so that that tall dude in front of you won’t ruin your movie watching experience.

 

Sporting Events

Fancy a baseball or football game out of the college league?

 

San Francisco Giants (Baseball)

Giants baseball might not be spectacular this season, but there’s always spring quarter. The King Street Caltrain station is a short walking distance from the park.

 

San Francisco 49ers (Football)

This could be their year. Football games are a great event to go with a group of friends and to get some excitement you can’t get in IHUM.

 

San Jose Sharks (Hockey)

The Sharks are picking up their game, making any hockey game more exciting

 

Oakland A’s (Baseball)

Similar to Giants, but like I said, maybe they’ll start on a better foot.

 

Oakland Raiders (Football)

Many people aren’t fans of the Raiders - they seem to collect old players from other teams - but you might have someone here to cheer for.

 

Golden State Warriors (Basketball)

Yet another Bay Area team that doesn’t seem to be doing so hot, but they have been better in recent seasons.
