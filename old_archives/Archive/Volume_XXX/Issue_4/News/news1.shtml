Change of the Guard for the ASSU
by Charles Hallford
News Staff

On April 9 and 10, Stanford's student government, the Associated Students of Stanford University (ASSU), held elections for the offices of ASSU president and vice-president; senior, junior, and sophomore class presidents; Graduate Student Council members, and undergraduate senators. Also on the ballot were the requests for Special Fees of 61 different student groups for the next school year.

Endorsed by the Stanford Daily, junior Nadiya Figueroa and sophomore Joey Natoli won the offices of ASSU president and vice-president, running on a slogan of "empowering student voice." The Fire It Up ballot, consisting of Shayna Fernandez, Mary Himinkool, Mikey Lee, and Ryan Taylor, won the offices of senior class presidents. The Juniorosity! ballot, consisting of Alex Bradford, Ebony Nyenya, Valerie Rozycki, and Subha Srinivas, won the offices of junior class presidents, and The '06 Pack ballot, consisting of Pete Borum, Victoria Carr, Stephanie Tsai, and Robert Walker, won the offices of Sophomore class presidents.

The student body voted to meet the special requests of all of the 61 candidate groups except for the Asia Technology Initiative (ATI), the Forum for America/Chinese Exchange at Stanford (FACES), the Stanford Archery Club, and the Stanford Harmonics.

Juniors Steve Yelderman and Charlie Stockman ran as the First Choice ballot against Miss Figueroa and Mr. Natoli, their sole opponents. The First Choice ballot received 1,503 votes whereas their opponents won with approximately 2,500 votes. The Stanford College Republicans endorsed the First Choice ballot even though Mr. Yelderman and Mr. Stockman ran on a very anti-war and pro-affirmative action platform.

While the victorious candidates have three years of ASSU experience between them, Mr. Yelderman's ASSU experience is limited to his current term as ASSU Publications Board director, and Mr. Stockman has not held any ASSU offices. In their campaigning, Mr. Yelderman and Mr. Stockman portrayed their relative inexperience positively by promising to widen the scope of the ASSU's role in student affairs, while Miss Figueroa and Mr. Natoli promised not to be limited by "in-the-box" thinking.

Mr. Stockman utilized more humorous antics by far in his campaign than did any other executive candidate. According to the First Choice platform as declared in the ASSU Elections Handbook, if elected Mr. Stockman would have use "utilitarian principles to calculate the maximum amount of pleasure that can be attained during his vice-presidency, and making all official decisions accordingly." On Monday April 7, a debate between executive candidates was held at the CoHo in Tresidder Student Union.

During this debate, Mr. Stockman used a large stuffed penis to illustrate characteristics he wanted to see in the new Dean of Students to replace outgoing Dean of Students Marc Wais. He stated, "[The new Dean of Students is] going to have to be firm with the student body but also be flexible, he'll have to have a good head on his shoulders, and he'll have to have serious cajones."

On April 9, the Stanford Daily ran a ten-page insert consisting of past articles concerning the elections, an endorsement of Miss Figueroa and Mr. Natoli, and recommendations on which student groups should receive Special Fees. On April 10, the Daily ran their elections recommendations yet again.

The Daily used a ranking system consisting of the three ranks: Yes, No, and Maybe. Out of only four student groups to be denied Special Fees in the actual election, two received a ranking of No and two received a ranking of Maybe. Out of the 59 student groups given a ranking by the Stanford Daily, 17 received a ranking of No, 15 a ranking of Maybe, and 27 a ranking of Yes.

On Friday April 11, a letter by Jamal Watkins Ô02, appeared in the Daily criticizing its special-fee endorsements by calling them a "type of biased, partisan lobbying." According to Mr. Watkins, "Journalistic integrity would seem to demand objective polling or a campus-wide review committee to assess all special fee groups, including the Daily."

Also on Friday, Kujtese "Tesa" Bejtulahu, co-director of the World Refugee Leadership Program, wrote to the Stanford Daily offering clarification of the purpose of the World Refugee Leadership Program because "of the Daily's failure to endorse the World Refugee Leadership Program's special-fee request."

Sophomore Robert McConnell wrote a letter to the Daily claiming, "The Daily's statements reflect an ignorance of [the Stanford Solar Car Project and the Stanford Undergraduate Research Journal] as well as a misunderstanding of the purpose and usefulness of ASSU special fees."
