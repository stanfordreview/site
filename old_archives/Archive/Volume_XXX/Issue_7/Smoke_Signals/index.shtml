Smoke Signals
Volume XXX in Review...
Joel Beinin You all may have noticed that Beinin Watch has been on hiatus as of late. Can it be that the good professor has made a turn for the better, or has he simply become better at covering his tracks? I can't answer this question, but I'm inclined to remain critical. After all, only an admission of wrong signals a turn toward right - I suspect that we shall hear again soon from Prof. Beinin.
Learning about Current Events Professors taking their classes on strike, Jesse Jackson running amok - if this is learning, I'm afraid that the teaching is being done in a language foreign to most notions of common sense. Add the Stanford Daily's ever-beneficent influence to this mix, and we have, as ever, a lot of work to do.
Student Government Thankfully, we find at least one bright spot: bureaucratic and misguided as it might be, the ASSU is finally picking up the slack on Special Fees.
"Diversity" The volume thus comes full-circle. Affirmative action, campus antisemitism, attempts to remove the Hoover - we have worked to expose layered fallacies and nuances complicating Stanford's so-called diversity. "Diversity" is an attractive ideal, but the very people who want it so badly condemn it to failure.
