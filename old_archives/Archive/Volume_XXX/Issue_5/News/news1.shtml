The News in Brief
Diversity Fest
The second annual Diversity Fest 2003, sponsored by Murray, the Comparative Studies in Race and Ethnicity dorm, took place on Friday, April 18. Murray's focus program was established in order to try to promote racial and ethnic understanding among residents and the Stanford community as a whole.

Diversity Fest began with a fair in White Plaza during which various student performing arts groups from the campus performed while students could engage in conversation and "see our diverse community in action!" The Fest concluded with a faculty panel, including visiting History Professor Gin Yong Pang and Professor of Sociology C. Matthew Snipp, featuring discussion of marginalized people within marginalized ethnic communities.

ASSU Reconsiders Special Fees Policy
The ASSU Senate discussed possible changes to the policy concerning special fees in response to the sizable increase in fees requested by student groups, mostly due to the increased number of groups requesting fees. Due primarily to student apathy, almost all student groups who apply for special fees receive the requested funds. In this year's election 57 of 61 groups requesting fees were approved.

ASSU Senators fear that the increase in funds per student, which will be $206 for the upcoming year, will cause more students to request special fee refunds, in addition to more publicity of the ability to withdraw funds. Proposed solutions include a possible cap placed on the amount of fees groups a student could vote to approve, stricter budget trimming processes, and more stringent requirements to apply for special fees. However, the ASSU has no current plans to alter the buffer system in which student groups receive the voted amount of money from a calculated overcharge of students no matter how many withdraw funds from the organization.

Stanford Bans Alcohol in All-Freshman Dorms
As a result of its annual review of the Alcohol Policy, Residential Education, the Dean of Student Affairs, the Dean of Freshm and and Transfer Students, the Department of Public Saftey, and Vaden Health Promotion decided to amend the University's alcohol policy by no longer allowing alcohol to be served at events in all-freshman halls, lounges, or outer areas. The new policy will take effect beginning next year, and is the first change in the policy since 1997 when the use of dorm funds for alcohol was prohibited.

Many students and staff believe the new policy will encourage behind-the-scenes drinking and thereby increase danger because of the lack of monitoring by other dorm residents and staff members. However, the administration argues that underage drinking is, in fact, illegal, and that excessive drinking in freshman dorms is a serious problem and they hope the new policy will reduce peer pressure and the number of students who drink underage.

Queer Awareness Days
The Lesbian, Gay, Bisexual, and Transgender Community Resources Center (LGBT-CRC) are sponsoring the annual celebration of queer issues known as Queer Awareness Days (QuAD). The events began April 16 and will last until April 30.

The first event was a National Day of Silence, in which participants protested the silently suffered intolerance and prejudice by not speaking and eating a silent lunch. The purpose of the nationwide event is to promote passage of nondiscrimination laws that include LGBT bias. Other scheduled events include staged play-readings, book-readings, cartoonist presentations, film screenings, musical performances, and political and social discussions.
