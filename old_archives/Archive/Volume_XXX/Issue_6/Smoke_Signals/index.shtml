Smoke Signals
We return to the perspective offered by Smoke Signals, where the week gets put at a safe distance....
Special Fees It's good to hear that the new chair of the Undergrad Senate is taking the concern of mounting Special Fees seriously, but it remains to be seen whether the 14 neophyte senators who serve as his colleagues will follow his lead.
Jesse Jackson Who are we kidding? He's not even amusing any more. Reverend Jackson's presence serves as an insistent reminder of two things: (1) we must always be vigilant against rabble-rousing, and (2) we must take care to be critical toward leftist conceptions of "diversity."
Spring Quarter The work accumulates, and the money dries up, yet the weather has only just begun to show signs of summer.
Vigil for Tolerance It is heartening to see that at least some Stanford students have a sense of respect for the past and are willing to learn from it.
Zbigniew Brzezinski Logical and methodical, Brzezinski's arguments painted a picture of a world increasingly dominated by the US and China. The great unknown--will China really be able and willing to understand the need to fight WMD proliferation and terrorist networks with all of its strength?
Letters to the Editor What happened to them? We can't have informed discussions if we don't have input from our readership. Please, write, write, and write more!
