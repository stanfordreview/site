Beinin Watch
Funding Anti-Americanism With American Money
Professor Joel Beinin from the Department of History
 Joel Beinin, Professor of Middle Eastern History, has been accused on numerous occasions of speaking out in support of terrorist organizations and of spearheading antisemitic practices at Stanford. This section is dedicated to keeping the Stanford community informed as to his actions in the hopes of forcing Professor Beinin to account for his actions.

Stanford Hoover Fellow Stanley Kurtz made a noteworthy comment in the July 22 issue of National Review on the federal government's increased funding of Middle East educational projects following the September 11th attacks on America. In an effort to develop American expertise on the languages and cultures of the Middle East, the federal government approved massive funding increases to academic "area studies" programs.

Ironically, this money has been used to line the pockets and promote the work of the very people most bitterly opposed to the War on Terror. As Kurtz notes, Joel Beinin's Middle East Studies Association (MESA) stands to benefit most from the funding windfall as the "scholars" associated with the organization receive larger research grants and departmental funding increases that will inevitably be used to promote anti-Israeli sentiment.

Mr. Beinin has an impressive list of academic credentials that would delight terrorists and their sympathizers. He proudly affiliates himself with such anti-Israeli groups as MESA, the Middle East Report, the Middle East Research and Information Project, and the Palestinian American Research Center -- all organizations that provide "education and information" about the Israel-driven plight of the Palestinians.

Perhaps the one organization that best exemplifies the admiration he has amongst anti-Semites is a pro-Palestinian terror Web site (Palestine: Information with Provenance) that claims to fight "the Zionist propaganda machine." The site presents material "intended to help people who, after a lifetime's indoctrination by Zionist propaganda, are starting to question their beliefs and who are interested in learning the truth." One of their heroes and contributors is Professor Beinin.

This is the man who says that "we should actively advocate the idea that lively discussion of Middle Eastern affairs, not slavish parroting. . . . is the best way to promote good public policy and an informed citizenry" while he simultaneously leads a boycott aimed at preventing universities and government institutions from hiring any pro-Israeli academics. For a man who said before September 11th that the notion of an "Islamic threat" was nothing but a scare tactic invented by the government to maintain excessively high defense budgets, we must question why government officials and even Stanford University are willing to support the genuine "uniformity threat" posed by radicals like Beinin.

-- Joe Fairbanks
