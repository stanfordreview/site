Stanford College Republicans Go to Statewide Convention
A group of about ten Stanford College Republicans headed to Sacramento for the California Republican Convention the weekend of February 21-23, attending the first convention since the group's reestablishment on campus at the beginning of this year. The event provided a unique opportunity to see politics first-hand, a chance to rub elbows with state officials, local candidates, and other notables in the Republican party such as Oakland NAACP chairman Shannon Reeves, and the occasion to meet other fellow College Republicans struggling amidst the bastions of liberality constituted by California universities. ....Full story.....by Travis Menk and Bob Sensenbrenner

"Regret to Inform" that Iraq and Vietnam Are Not the Same Conflict
On Thursday, February 27, Narquis Barak, a visiting lecturer from Harvard University, and several graduate students in Stanford's Communications Department showed the film "Regret to Inform." The film is part of a series entitled the "Peace-Oriented Film" series. Sponsored by Stanford's CASA Program and Communications Department, the series is part of a concerted effort by on-campus liberal academics to encourage student opposition to potential conflict in Iraq. ....Full story.....by Harrison Osaki
