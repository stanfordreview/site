The News In Brief
Educational Equity Freebake
The Students for Educational Equity sponsored Freebake/Counterbake 2003 last week, distributing free baked goods every day from noon until one in White Plaza. The slogan of the event was "Education is a Right, Not a Privilege." The purpose of the event was to entice students to come for the free food, then stay to discuss affirmative action and its role in the "Struggle for Equality."

Among the daily themes was "Bamboozled! What they won't tell you at that other bake sale," referencing the proposed bake sale by the Stanford College Republicans which would offer baked goods at cheaper prices for minority students. However, instead of charging for their baked goods, the Freebake offered free cookies, brownies, and other sweets, claiming that "You can't put a price on these baked goods and you can't put a price on education."

ASSU Recommends Meal Plan/Laundry Changes
The ASSU passed two resolutions this week in relation to student meal plans and student laundry fees. The bill related to meal plans supports the Stanford Dining Committee's recommendation for a new option of ten meals and 35 points per week. This plan option was introduced because the current cheapest meal plan is 14 meals per week, which many students do not use. However, the new option will result in a $17 or $27 increase in the price of other meal plans in addition to the planned 3% increase in all meal plans.

The laundry bill suggests instituting an in-house laundry system to replace the outsourcing to a for-profit company. The plan would also include charging students for laundry per-quarter instead of per-load, allowing students unlimited access to the laundry machines. Both the meal plan and the laundry bills are part of the ASSU's focus on reducing the cost of living on campus.

Diversity Action Council Suffers Budget Cuts
The Diversity Action Council consists of four subcommittees representing faculty, staff, undergraduate students, and graduate students, each of which developed proposals over the past year that were submitted to Provost Etchemendy last fall. Despite budget cuts and hiring freezes due to the downturn in the economy, the council is proceeding with most of the recommendations, devising alternatives, or developing new ideas. Etchemendy affirms that, despite the budget cuts, the University's commitment to diversity is strong.

Included among the proposals by the subcommittees are the adoption of search committees for minority faculty and the improvement of advising and mentoring programs for undergraduates. The subcommittee for staff proposed the creation of a career advising center for staff, but it cannot currently be afforded. The graduate student subcommittee proposed the creation of a Vice Provost for Graduate Student Diversity, which was ruled out. Instead, the job description of Associate Vice Provost for Policy Mungal will be redefined to include promoting diversity among graduate students.

Office for Religious Life Supports Queer Marriages
The Queer/Straight Social and Political Alliance (QSSPA) sponsored "Stanford Freedom to Marry Day" on Thursday, February 26, in White Plaza. QSSPA and Senior Associate Dean for Religious Life, Rabbi Patricia Karlin-Neumann, performed four mock wedding ceremonies, including same-sex unions. The purpose of the event was to raise awareness about the illegality of same-sex marriages.

The mock weddings featured wedding cake and music provided by the Leland Stanford Junior University Marching Band. A petition was passed around at the event advocating the support by the ASSU of queer marriage. In the evening, a wedding reception was held in Tresidder celebrating the fight for the right to gay marriages featuring food, music, and sign-coloring. All events were co-sponsored by the ASSU and Queerillas.

The Office for Religious Life officially supports same-sex unions, believing in the affirmation of love and establishment of long-term commitment. The Office for Religious Life has in the past conducted multiple commitment ceremonies for gay and lesbian couples in Memorial Church.
