Hennessy Talks Affirmative Action
Full Transcript
On Thursday, February 27, Stanford President John Hennessy granted the Stanford Review an exclusive interview on the topic of affirmative action. The university president offered a positive assessment of Stanford's policy with regard to admissions and faculty hiring. However, the interview included a number of contradictory statements that, far from closing the book on the issue, may fuel further controversy. Arguing that affirmative action is key to diversity, the president suggested that its practice must continue. At the same time, however, he assessed Stanford to have achieved a near-optimal level of diversity. ....Full story in Front Page.....by Laura Billadello

Stanford College Republicans Go to Statewide Convention
A group of about ten Stanford College Republicans headed to Sacramento for the California Republican Convention the weekend of February 21-23, attending the first convention since the group's reestablishment on campus at the beginning of this year. Viennese Ball Brings Joy of Tradition
The princess gowns, ice sculptures, and myriad flowers donning the ballroom of the Hyatt Regency, Burlingame during Stanford's 26th annual Viennese Ball provided a stark contrast to the typical Stanford party. Nonetheless, over four hundred couples crowded the dance floor on Friday, February 21, continuing a tradition that has grown more than threefold since the first ball was held in 1978. In winter of that year, students returning from the Stanford-in-Vienna program attempted to emulate the frequent and popular balls held throughout Austria during Fasching, the festive period between New Year's Eve and Ash Wednesday. They staged the ball in the Toyon Hall lounge and attracted roughly three hundred students. In 1998, the ball was moved to an off-campus location to accommodate the burgeoning number of attendees and since then has taken place at several different Bay Area hotels. Scores of students look forward to the event as the highlight of winter quarter, if not the entire academic year. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Hillary Connell
