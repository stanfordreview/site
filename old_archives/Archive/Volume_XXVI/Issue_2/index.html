Labour Likely To Land On Its Feet
We on the greener side of the pond are not holding our breath. The upcoming British general election results are more of a foregone conclusion than the election date: Labour will almost certainly win but when exactly it will be is debatable, as is the prospect that recent holes punched in the Government's legitimacy and voter apathy will combine to put a dent in Labour's hefty majority. The Conservative party is about as likely to win this election as Al Gore is to tuck his tail between his legs and recede from American politics into an island of cool academia. .....Full story in News.....by Brooke Brody-Waite

Graffiti Not A Hate Crime
Sometime between March 14th and March 20th, some idiot decided to scribble some nonsense on some classroom walls. Had the content of the nonsense been something about cartoon characters or invisible dog fences or sociological organization theory, no one would have cared. The scribbles were not comments on any of those things; they were some asinine rants about racial minorities. Nothing on the walls had much of a coherent thought to it, and everything seemed sort of foolish and banal. .....Full story in Opinion .....by Jeffrey Davidson

Davis's Electricity Policy Deplorable
A summer of rolling blackouts threatens to seriously damage California's economy. Governor Gray Davis presents this as the lesser of two evils, with the alternative of letting the retail price of electricity rise until the resulting conservation brings demand into line with supply. Davis rejects this market approach on the grounds that it would force Californians to pay gouger's prices. He has grudgingly accepted a mere 40% rate increase after wholesale prices have gone up 700% from last year. By summer prices are expected to be up 1300% from last year, and they have already spiked as high as 5000%. .....Full story in Opinion.....by Alec Rawls
