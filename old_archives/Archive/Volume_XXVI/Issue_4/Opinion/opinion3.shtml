Opinion
Compassionate Conservatism Quiz
by Scott Rasmussen
Editorial Staff

It is no coincidence that President Bush chose to break tradition and pay the first state visit of his administration to Mexico, not Canada. Mexico and its recently elected president, Vicente Fox of the center-right Party for National Action (PAN), will find over the coming years that they have more in common with President Bush than his interests in Spanish and cowboy hats. The recent commitment of the 34 Western hemisphere democracies to create a regional free trade area by 2005 should provide ample opportunity for the NAFTA partners to work together, and Bush's strategy of looking south to Mexico to help address the U.S.'s current energy woes will require an unprecedented degree of cooperation between the two countries.

The ideological similarity between Bush and Fox should certainly help to smooth any bumps along the way. Add the extra Ôc' of cooperation to Bush's mantra of compassionate conservatism, and you have a pretty good definition of what it means to be a "new conservative" in North America. In fact, the rhetoric in the speeches that each president gave after 100 days in office is so similar that I have a test for our readers: identify who said what. Below are excerpts from Bush and Fox on the major political themes of today; answers to the quiz follow at the end. Just to make it more difficult, we've eliminated the obvious clues of language and geographic references. Enjoy!

1. CHANGING THE TONE

What have we accomplished so far? I think we're making progress toward changing the tone. Bitterness and divisiveness in [capital] poison the mood of the whole country. On the other hand, a culture of respect and results in [capital] can change the mood of the country for the better.

During these first 100 days we have guided our actions with a new ethic of government guided by transparency, legality, and tolerance. We have not disappointed the immense trust that has been given to us by misusing our power in partisan battles.

2. ECONOMIC REFORM

Only by changing our tax structure will we put [country] in the place we want to be. Creating well-paying jobs is the most effective formula for economic growth.

Tax relief is an important part of our economic strategy. It will accelerate our economic growth and create more jobs and more opportunity.

3. NATIONALISM

The spirit of cooperation that started with my election will push [country] forward to the proud future that we deserve; this is my most important goal as president.

This has nothing to do with me or my political party. It has everything to do with what is right for the country.

4. ENVIRONMENTAL POLICY

We're at work on a plan to increase the energy supply of [country] in the long-term. At the same time, we are acting in a common sense way to defend our environment.

Irrational and excessive use of our natural resources has sent us down a dangerous road. We need to define a new relationship between the environment and economic activity.

5. POLITICAL DIALOGUE

Internationally, we are building a more peaceful and open world . . . But it's just as important for us to listen as it is to speak . . . I said my piece, and I listened, as well. That's how good neighbors behave.

I am convinced that dialogue is the essence of government. We are developing new initiatives to facilitate understanding and coordination between the three branches of government.

6. OPTIMISM

These aren't just dreams; instead, we are committed to diligently working to transform our vision into reality: so that each day will be better than the last, so that our every step brings us closer to a better future. For the first in many years there is hope of better days to come. The majority of [country's] people are excited about it. To my fellow country men and women, I promise you, I will not fail. Thank you very much.

In nearly 100 days, we have made a good start. But it's only a start. On a number of important issues we have laid the foundation for progress. Now we need to turn a good start and good spirit into good laws. And I urge [legislature] to join me in seizing the opportunities of the next 100 days and beyond. Thank you for listening.

ANSWERS
1. Bush, Fox 2. Fox, Bush 3. Fox, Bush
4. Bush, Fox 5. Bush, Fox 6. Fox, Bush

Full texts of the respective speeches of Presidents Bush and Fox are available online at www.whitehouse.gov and www.presidencia.gob.mx.

Scott Rasmussen is a sophomore majoring in Economics and Mathematical and Computational Science.
