University Not A Political Institution
If you look closely at a Stanford athlete at work, you will notice a little mark on each article of clothing s/he is wearing. That mark is the Nike swoosh. The reason all of our athletes wear the swoosh is that Nike pays for all of our athletes' clothing. So wait, we get thousands and thousands of dollars worth of free athletic gear, and in return, all we need is to deal with a little check mark that comes on those thousands of dollars in gear? Only among Stanford liberals could this seem like a bad deal. .....Full story.....by Jeff Davidson

Compassionate Conservatism Quiz
It is no coincidence that President Bush chose to break tradition and pay the first state visit of his administration to Mexico, not Canada. Mexico and its recently elected president, Vicente Fox of the center-right Party for National Action (PAN), will find over the coming years that they have more in common with President Bush than his interests in Spanish and cowboy hats. The recent commitment of the 34 Western hemisphere democracies to create a regional free trade area by 2005 should provide ample opportunity for the NAFTA partners to work together, and Bush's strategy of looking south to Mexico to help address the U.S.'s current energy woes will require an unprecedented degree of cooperation between the two countries. .....Full story.....by Scott Rasmussen
