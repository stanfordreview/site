Conference on the Second Amendment
A conference at the law school scrutinized the 2nd amendment on April 21. Professors from multiple disciplines gathered to discuss the right to bear arms from a historical, legal, and cultural perspective. The event kicked off on Friday, April 20 with the keynote address, "How Americans Because an Armed People", given by Emory University professor Michael A. Bellesiles. Stanford professor Jack Rakove headed the event. Fellow Stanford faculty Pam Karlan and Robert Weisberg also spoke. Though many students and professors appreciated the event as an opportunity to examine the issue and promote discussion of the controversial 2nd amendment, not all members of the Stanford community welcomed it. At least one law student accused a conference participant of fraudulent research.

Language Departments to Merge
Other students are up in arms concerning the proposed language department merger. The administration announced last month that it was considering merging six language departments into one, to be called the "Division of Literatures, Cultures and Languages." Although it claims that the move is motivated purely out of organizational concerns, many undergraduate and graduate students are not happy about the proposal. Many feel that it will devalue each of the individual apartments. Although the merger will create five new faculty positions, many students feel that this will only create tension between the sub-departments - since there is one more department than there will be new positions. Other students also feel that this is a further manifestation of the university's trend towards devaluing the humanities curriculum in general. Students who feel particularly strong about the matter silently protested outside the faculty senate meeting last Thursday afternoon.

Earth Week at Stanford
However, proving that they can cooperate too, faculty and students worked together last week to promote Earth Week at Stanford. Conveniently juxtaposed with California's ever maddening energy crisis, Earth Week brought together organizers and volunteers who presented speakers, organized demonstrations, and promoted discussion about the environment. In White Plaza on Thursday afternoon, students implored the Stanford Community to conserve energy and recycle. Nearby, in front of Tresidder, several electric cars were modeled as examples of how we can combat the problem of conspicuous oil consumption in the future. Keynote speaker Amory Lovins topped off the event on Thursday evening, speaking to a large and anticipatory crowd on the topic of capitalism and the environment.
