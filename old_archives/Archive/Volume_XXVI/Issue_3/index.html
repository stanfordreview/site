White Plaza is never quiet from noon to one--that is the only hour of the day during which students can hold vocal demonstrations. Some student groups apparently feel that if they don't demonstrate every day they'll lose that privilege forever. So no matter how placid the campus political scene seems to be from the outside, somebody will find something to yell about. .....Full story in Editorial Commentary.....by Matthew Barrett

Tax Cut Necessary For Economic Vitality
The American public (hopefully) finished thinking about taxes as they mailed and e-filed returns by this year's April 16 deadline, but for politicians, tax season is only beginning. At stake is the ten-year $1.6 trillion tax cut centerpiece of President George W. Bush's budget, which has been approved by the House of Representatives but which appears doomed to concessions in the evenly-divided Senate. Americans should let their representatives in Congress know that supporting a tax cut--one that's big enough to make a difference--is the best choice they can make to encourage fiscal responsibility, to promote economic recovery, and to return power to the American people. .....Full story in Opinion.....by Scott Rasmussen

Sullivan Talks About Gays And The Catholic Church
On Friday April 13th Andrew Sullivan, a noted social and political commentator and a former editor of The New Republic, delivered a campus lecture entitled "The Politics of Homosexuality" His lecture was the keynote event for Stanford's Queer Awareness Days (QUAD) which took place from April 2-13 and also featured events by comedian Kate Clinton and documentary filmmaker Tom Shepard. .....Full story in News.....by Kathryn Davidovich

Lovins Praises Capitalism
Last Thursday night, "Earthweek" keynote speaker Amory Lovins expressed the hope that members of his audience would "organize the non-violent overthrow of bad engineering." Addressing a packed auditorium as part of a benefit for Palo Alto's Ohlone Elementary School District, Mr. Lovins advanced the theme of a book he recently co-authored: "Natural Capitalism: The Next Industrial Revolution." Mr. Lovins, a Harvard and Oxford educated experimental physicist, author or co-author of 27 books, MacArthur Fellow, environmental-issues speaker, and business consultant, was sponsored by a myriad of groups both within and without the Stanford community, including SEAS, whose mission statement among other things declares that "we cannot really address root causes unless we confront the racist power structures, capitalism, and corporatization that surrounds us." .....Full story in News.....by Alex Robbins

Boykin Speaks Out On Oppression of Blacks, Gays
Activist Keith Boykin appeared at the Law School on April 9 to talk about his experience with discrimination as both a black man and a gay man. Before a receptive crowd, he spoke about gays in the military, gays and Christianity, and the relationship between the struggle for gay rights and the struggle for black rights. .....Full story in News.....by Christopher Fish
