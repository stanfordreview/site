Trust Parents, Not Politics
A liberal society embraces the priority of liberty. It acknowledges that voluntary arrangements are inherently superior to force, both morally and practically, implying that government (as one embodiment of force) should only restrict liberty (the prerequisite to voluntary agreement) for reasons important enough to outweigh the very great value of liberty. In the case of our nation's socialist monopoly on education, the proposed justifications for public monopoly--to insure quality educational opportunities for all, to promote integration and common bonds--are far better served by systems of parental choice. Thus at best the socialist monopoly is an inadvertent illiberalism. Those who support it could perhaps be imagining that the value of liberty has been outweighed, when actually it has been augmented. .....Full story in Essay.....by Alec Rawls
