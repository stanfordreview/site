Daily Made Wrong Decision
The now notorious ad, "Ten Reasons Why Reparations for Blacks is a Bad Idea for Blacks--and Racist Too," offered to college newspapers around the country, at last made it to Stanford (sort of). The advertisement was sent to numerous papers by David Horowitz's Center for the Study of Popular Culture. Many of them refused to run it, a few ran it and apologized, and a very few stood by the First Amendment and published it without apology. The Stanford Daily, cognizant of the controversy the ad elicited at other schools, attempted the interesting strategy of censoring it in the name of promoting discussion. .....Full story in Opinion.....by Henry Towsner

Letter to the Editor
Dear Editor,

Recently, I was walking down the hall in my residence when I noticed that many doors had identical and seemingly handwritten notes taped onto them. Looking closer, I noticed that they were photocopied and that they were placed by SEAS (Students for Environmental Action at Stanford) in order to present the organization's opposition to the construction of California's 34th prison in Delano. As a self-described moderate, I like to read the opinions of SEAS, as they have often presented many valid arguments in favor of reasonable and worthwhile causes in the past.
