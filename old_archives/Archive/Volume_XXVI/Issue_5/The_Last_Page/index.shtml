The Last Page
Political Philosophy According To The Stanford Draw
Socialism: Axess assigns you a draw number. The Administration/ASSU (hereafter referred to as "The Man") lowers it if it's high, raises it if it's low, and reduces everyone to the mediocrity that is Wilbur.

Leninism: Axess assigns you a draw number. The Man shoots you and collectivizes the extra housing space.

Maoism: Axess assigns you a draw number. Be careful to keep your belongings at least a uniform distance away from your new neighbors' rooms, or you may have to resort to weeks of negotiations and media sensationalism to bring them home.

Democracy: Axess assigns you a draw number. Vote with your fellow students to revolt against The Man and instead distribute housing indirectly through representatives with massive special-interest war chests.

Florida Democracy: Axess assigns you a draw number. You are too stupid to fill out your residence choices correctly. Jesse Jackson comes to town to defend your rights.

Capitalism: Axess assigns you a draw number. Sell it to the spoiled rich Stanford student of your choice, and use the proceeds to try to cover the annual 5% tuition increase.

Anarchism: Axess assigns you a draw number. You get a 1. Your life is solitary, nasty, brutish, and short.

Multiculturalism: Axess assigns you a draw number. Every house has been transformed into a theme dorm, so be prepared for the conformity of political correctness.

Libertarianism: Axess assigns you a draw number. It bugs you a little that your neighbors are using their 400 for Stern, but, hey, it's a free country.

Progressivism: Axess assigns you a draw number. Activists attempt to shame you into believing that the only cure for slums like Mirrielees is giving more power to The Man.

Conservatism: Axess assigns you a draw number. Let them live in the Mirrielees slums; you aren't going to support The Man's Capital Improvement Program.

Compassionate Conservatism: Axess assigns you a draw number. You empathize with the residents of the Mirrielees slums, and you defer the solution to private faith-based initiatives.

Vegetarianism: Axess assigns you a draw number. You live in a co-op.

Utilitarianism: Axess assigns you a draw number. Fortunately, it is 3000, for your society gives the greatest good for the greatest number.

Realism: Axess assigns you a draw number. Doesn't matter to much too you, though, since you piled up a dozen priorities "just in case."

Surrealism: Axess assigns you a peanut. You sing to the chameleon.

Plagiarism: Axess assigns you a draw number. It inspires a brilliant rip-off of a joke about cows.
