Bacon Blames NAFTA For Harming Rights
On February 27, freelance photojournalist David Bacon came to Stanford to speak about NAFTA, globalization, and the Mexican labor movement. His appearance was sponsored by the Stanford Labor Action Coalition, and during his presentation he showed students numerous slides documenting the condition of Mexican workers both in Mexico and the United States. As Mr. Bacon presented these slides, he gave a first-hand narrative about each one, depicting for the assembled students both the harsh poverty under which many Mexican workers live as well as their attempts to ameliorate their condition through labor activism and protest. .....Full story.....by Alex Robbins

Rhode Speaks On Women And Leadership
Deborah Rhode blamed unfair expectations, lack of opportunity, and lack of social support for women's inability to attain equality with men in the workplace. For most of recorded history, she said, women have been inferior to men. And, although traditional gender roles have changed in recent years, women are still far behind. .....Full story.....by Christopher Fish

Conference at Tresidder Promotes Electoral Reform in California
How well does our election system really work? That was the question asked by many of the attendees of the California Electoral Reform Conference, held on Saturday, March 03, 2001 in the Oak Room at Tresidder. Among the groups that played an active role in the conference were the California Instant-Runoff Voting Coalition and the Center for Voting and Democracy, both which support similar reforms in the electoral process. .....Full story.....Justin Diener
