This Week's Events

Forward Article to a Friend

The Review continues its new weekly list of upcoming events--including popular parties, interesting lectures, and important campus events.

Event Time and Place

Description
Regional Dragon Boat Regatta Sat, May 13, 11am-3pm at Paragon Point (1 Lagoon Dr, Redwood City, CA 94065) The Stanford dragon boat team (“Terminal Velocity”) will be competing with other bay area teams. The team consists of over a dozen rowers and a drummer, to keep beat.
Ah-i-Nafs Music Concert and Falafel Sat, May 13, 3:30pm at Bechtel International Center “Their music combines elements of jazz improvization, funk, and electronic dance music with percussion,vocals and other samples of world music from the Middle East, North Africa, India, and Sub-Saharan Africa. Free falafal will be provided.”
Barry Bixler: Engineering Design and Analysis of the Speedo FSII Swimsuit Tues, May 16, 4:15pm-5:15pm at Building 300, Room 300
 Barry Bixler of Honeywell Aerospace will be discussing the design olympic swimsuits. He will discuss how fluid mechanics and aerodynamics inspired the Speedo FS11, the most successful swimsuit in Athens 2004.
The Politics of History: the Case of Japan and Korea at Philippines Wed, May 17, 2006, 12:00pm-1:30pm at Conference Room, 3rd floor of Encina Hall Kan Kimura, a professor at Kobe University, will discuss the post-war political relationship between Japan and Korea.
VegFest Thurs, May 18, 11:00am-1:00pm in White Plaza VegFest is a vegan food festival, where students can get free vegan dishes from businesses and local restaurants. It encourages students to consider vegan or vegetarian eating options.
The Da Vinci Code & Jesus - Sorting Facts from Fiction Fri, May 19, 7-8:30pm at 300-300 (Main Quad)
 John Ortberg a teaching pastor of Menlo Park Presbyterian Church, will discuss The Da Vinci Code. Ortberg plans to address many of the theories in the book, including the propositions that Jesus was married.
Brainstorms: New Frontiers in Science and Technology - “Testing Einstein in Space: The Gravity Probe B Mission” Thurs, May 18 7:30-9pm at Hewlett Teaching Center “Join Professor Francis Everitt for a lively explanation of GP-B’s development. No scientific knowledge is necessary—just a keen imagination and the love of a good story.”
Exotic Erotic Fri, May 19, late evening at 680 Lomita
 The famed party returns to Stanford. As usual, guys will be limited to one article of clothing—and girls two. Guests can expect to wait in line for quite sometime, although most find the adventure worthwhile.
