Kudos to the ASSU

Congratulations to the ASSU Undergraduate Senate for their recent decision not to endorse the Coalition Against Proposition 54, a collection of student oranizations organized to campaign against Prop. 54. We at the Review believe Proposition 54 is a great idea and would be a huge blow to institutionalized racism and a giant step towards a truly colorblind government which would hopefully be followed by a truly colorblind society. However, that is not the main reason why we are glad the ASSU decided the way it did. We believe any official ASSU endorsement of a politically controversial position such as Proposition 54 is extremely detrimental to the student body as a whole because of the division it causes among students and the marginalization of those in the minority, which is why such endorsements were prohibited in the ASSU Constitution. While the proposed bill was for endorsement of the Coalition and not the actual position, an endorsement of a student group designed explicitly to promote a political position is tantamount to endorsing the position. We are pleased that this year's Senate decided to abide by that rule. Moreover, we believe that endorsing a particular student group which is politically active would have been a dangerous precedent to set. Had the ASSU endorsed this Coalition, there would have been no grounds for denying an endorsement to any student group who requested it, including anything from affirmative action bake sales to Chicano/a power rallies. Therefore, kudos to the ASSU for getting this one right.
