Letter to the Editor
I find it sad that biased students at Stanford organize "Solar Energy Now" to support the Kyoto treaty, believing it to be a mechanism to solve or slow down what they consider to be man-made global warming. They have not done their homework! Kyoto would, if implemented, decrease "smokestack" gases from industrialized nations and give free reign to emissions from less productive regions, but have only a very minor effect on global warming.

Why do I say this?

Global warming (and cooling) is a product of geological changes that have been recorded in the rocks for over the past billion years. Indeed, during the past two million years there have been over thirty periods of warming and cooling. These changes have been deduced from studies of differences in oxygen isotopes in marine sediments, ice cores, tree ring measurements of radioactive carbon, microscopic life on seafloors, pollen in lake beds, mineral deposits in caves, etc. These measurable changes predate the industrial revolution and were not caused by man.

Some primary causes of global warming/cooling appear to be related to:

1. Cyclic changes in the amount of the sun's radiation that reaches the earth as influenced by changes in the earth's axis tilt and its orbit.

2. Episodic volcanism adding CO2 to the air.

3. Changes in the direction and temperature of the earth's oceans.

4. Changes in the sun's magnetism, which raises or lowers the heat coming to the earth.

There is a mountain of evidence that indicates warming and cooling periods are the result of natural phenomena and man has little control over them. It is unfortunate that the media, politicians, many world leaders, and "protect the earth" advocates do little homework and much prefer to blather unscientific nonsense rather than accept the fact that man has little control over climate.

- Glenn Waterman
Professor of Engineering, Stanford '33
