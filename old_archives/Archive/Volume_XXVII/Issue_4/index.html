Five Students Arrested in Labor Dispute
On Thursday, November 29, five students were arrested at the Stanford Hospital while protesting the hospital's subcontracting policy. According to an e-mail circulated by the Stanford Labor Action Coalition (SLAC) "8 students confronted (VP of General Services) Lou Saksen in his office, demanding that the (hospital)" refrain from subcontracting out 10 housekeeping positions." ....Full story in Front Page....by Matt Barrett

Stanford Clergy Discuss Terrorism, War
Following the September 11th attacks, members of the Stanford community turned to religion for comfort and mourning. In the weeks that followed, students turned their attention toward contemplation and discussion of the issues surrounding the tragedy. Religious leaders responded with their opinions on what we need to understand and focus on in this difficult situation. Their comments address both spiritual and practical concerns regarding our response to the terrorists. ....Full story in Front Page.....by Joe Lonsdale

A Word From The Editor
Three weeks ago, the Undergraduate Senate of the Associated Students of Stanford University (ASSU) passed its first political resolution since being constitutionally banned from doing so after a controversial statement against the Gulf War. The Stanford Review published a petition in the last issue which stated, in part, "While many Stanford students do support affirmative action, many do not. For the Senate to claim to speak for all students without even considering the dissenting opinions within its constituency is dishonest and disrespectful." ....Full story in Editor's Note

Solar Energy Now Group Works to Change Stanford Energy Policy
California alone produces more carbon dioxide per year than Germany. Approximately 85% of America's current energy needs are met with the conventional nonrenewable resources of petroleum, natural gas, and coal. Each of these sources emits carbon dioxide upon combustion. The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) has published reports that predict an increase in average global temperature anywhere from 1.4 to 5.8 oC. This explains much of what is driving the students involved with Solar Energy Now. ....Full story in News.....by Mark Zavislak

Californians to Walk for Capitalism
If you see a group of people carrying signs demanding more freedom and less government on December 2nd at noon, do not be surprised. These are the participants in the first ever global Walk for Capitalism. The event will take place in every major city around the world, including countries like Russia, China and Germany. Silicon Valley participants will congregate at the Tech Museum of innovation and walk to the City hall, 2 miles away. ....Full story in News.....by Topsy St. Matthew-Daniel

Is Christianity a Socialist Religion?
A close of mine recently referred to Christianity as a "socialist religion." As I recall, some famous theological professor also once claimed that "any serious Christian must be a socialist." Even Tony Blair, now the U.S. Ambassador-at-Large for the war effort, on September 22 of last year referred to "individuals who saw a connection between the values of Christ and the values of socialism and who chose to work out these connections in the rough and tumble of party politics." ....Full story in Opinion.....by Alex Robbins

Liberty Demands Religious Judgement
Given the high profile of pacifist and moral relativist ideas in academia, many students may imagine that our nation's ideals of religious toleration are based on a conviction that religious views are beyond the scope of moral judgement. In fact, the opposite is true. Ideals of toleration are not based on forbearance of judgement but from the submission of religion to human moral judgement. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Alec Rawls

W. Glenn Campbell
W. Glenn Campbell was the energizer supreme who, as director of the Hoover Institution on War, Revolution and Peace for 30 years, brought to the think-tank such intellectual luminaries as Robert Conquest, the historian; Milton Friedman, Nobel laureate and one of the world's greatest economists; Sidney Hook, the philosopher; George Shultz, former secretary of state; and Edward Teller, father of the hydrogen bomb. Wrote the London Economist: The Hoover Institution "is hard to match for sheer intellectual firepower." The Economist verdict was based on its survey of the world's think-tanks and Hoover got the highest score. The Soviet Union, in its unique fashion, also recognized Hoover's firepower. It denounced Hoover's scholars and scholarship and, in typical conspiratorial fashion, even blamed then-President Reagan's anti-Soviet foreign policies on the Hoover Institution. ....Full story in Obituary

Letter to the Editor
Sara Ines Calderon is behind the times (Letter, Oct. 11). Her self-righteous support for "grape activists" based unthinkingly on trite union fictions simply doesn't cut it. It is insulting to depict intelligent, independent, conscientious workers as merely helpless victims, slaving away "in the blazing sun, surrounded by toxic pesticides, for wages that could barely sustain a Stanford student," etc., even while thousands flock here to do just that year after year. (The only horrors she leaves out are the absurd claims of "working without water" or "women having to sleep with bosses," neither of which have ever been the rule. And a farmer is now fined $750 for not having toilet paper available.) Besides, the pesticides are the same as those used at Stanford, where she presumably goes willingly to class. ....Full story in Letter to the Editor
