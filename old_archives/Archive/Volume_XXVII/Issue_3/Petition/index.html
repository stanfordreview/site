Petition
Petition to the ASSU
We are disappointed that the Undergraduate Senate of the Associated Students of Stanford University has chosen to formally endorse affirmative action in such a pointless and ill-considered way.

While many Stanford students do support affirmative action, many do not. For the Senate to claim to speak for all students without even considering the dissenting opinions within its constituency is dishonest and disrespectful.

There was no reason for the Senate to pass this bill. Stanford is not reconsidering its affirmative action policies. Nothing was accomplished because the Senate offered its support. No opinions have been altered, no one has withdrawn their opposition. The Senate has merely wasted a few hours and disenfranchised those students who do not actively support affirmative action.

During their campaigns, the current senators promised such things as a new student union, better advising, increased Marguerite hours, more parking, new restaurants in Tresidder, new sources of funding for student groups, and a student on the Board of Trustees. The Senate should deliver these--or at least one of these--before wasting time on political grandstanding.

To sign or obtain a copy of this petition, e-mail the Editor of the Stanford Review at editor@stanfordreview.org.
