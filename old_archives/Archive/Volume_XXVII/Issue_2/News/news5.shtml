Cardinal Sports Roundup
Stanford Varsity Field Hockey Defeats Pacific
Stanford's Field Hockey dominated Pacific at the October 14 home game. Erica Swesey's hat trick and Rebecca Shapiro's shutout contributed to the 3-0 Cardinal win. Previously, Stanford defeated California 1-0 in its opener, ruining their 14-game regular season winning streak. The triumph over Pacific marked Stanford's third straight victory this season and confirmed Stanford's first place ranking in the NorPac West Division. The team aims to continue its winning streak on October 20 at Pacific.

Stanford Men's and Women's Soccer Ranked in Top 10
Both men's and women's soccer have performed impressively thus far in the season. As of Thursday, both had 9-1-0 records. The men lost to UCLA in a conference game, and are currently ranked 5th in the national polls. The women are ranked 8th in polls, having dropped one game to Santa Clara.

Stanford Football Loses in Battle of Undefeateds
Stanford football suffered its first loss this season to undefeated Washington State, on Saturday, October 13th. The Cardinal went down 45-39, even as Stanford Senior Brian Allen rushed 133 yards to score three touchdowns.

Junipero wins Phi Psi 500 Tug-of-War
Last Saturday October 13 in the belly of dry Lake Lag, HPAC Chris Folkman and three freshman teammates representing Junipero defeated teams from Roble, CS106, and Lambda Nu twice in succession in winning a tug-of-war competition sponsored by the Phi Psi fraternity. Along with winning a free dinner for 10 at Compadre's, Junipero also helped the event raise an estimated total of $1000 for the Urban Ministry of Palo Alto, a resource for homeless people in Palo Alto.

Compiled by William Rothacker, Chris Lin, Sam Shapero, and Jeremy Friedman.
