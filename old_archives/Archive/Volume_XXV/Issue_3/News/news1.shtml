Stanford's General Use Permit Causes Controversy
The controversy over Stanford's General Use Permit flared up again. Santa Clara County Supervisor Joe Simitian proposed that Stanford set aside one acre of land as off-limits for development for each 2,000 square feet it develops. In the meeting over the General Use Permit held Monday, October 30th, the University bused in students and provided them with dinner in order to make sure to get strong student turnout. Over 100 students, faculty, and staff who supported the University’s position attended out of the 300 total spectators. The final vote was taken October 31st.

ASSU Starts "Word On The Street"
Every Thursday in White Plaza, the ASSU hopes students will come together to participate "in a thought provoking discussion on culture, race, or politics." The first Word On The Street, on the election, was canceled due to rain, but the ASSU will continue the event. From the event forward; "Voice your opinion about those issues that are important to you. Further embrace the diversity around you and represent what you stand for."

Gore Campaign Goes Nasty
The Gore campaign called voters in Michigan, using a recorded conversation that blames the death of the husband of a Texas woman, Ann Friday, on Mr. Bush's failure to improve nursing home care. He died "from an illness that his nursing home attendants failed to notice." In another, a woman from Houston, Charlotte Cherry, says of Mr. Bush's "terrible record on the environment": "Governor Bush has accepted $1.3 million from corporate polluters, and he's allowed them to keep polluting while my kids suffer." In Wisconsin, the Gore campaign has aired ads which claim that the Wall Street Journal says that George W.'s plan would not work, a claim the Journal definitively refutes.
