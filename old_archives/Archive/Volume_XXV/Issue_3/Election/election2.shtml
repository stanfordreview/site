Election
The Libertarian Alternative
The "major parties" have offered you a choice this year. Do you want tax cuts that endorse behaviors Democrats like, or tax cuts that endorse behaviors Republicans like? Do you want the Internet filtered to eliminate content the Republicans find offensive or to eliminate content the Democrats find offensive? Should George W. Bush tell you how to live your life, or should it be Al Gore who does that?

Of course, maybe you want to make decisions for yourself. Maybe you believe that the first amendment means that you should decide what to view on the Internet, not your neighbor. Maybe you think taxes should be used to raise funds rather than as cattle prods to manipulate people. Maybe you remember the Federalist Papers' warning about the tyranny of the majority and would rather not be babied by a government which believes that you will ruin yourself if not told what to do.

If so, Harry Browne and Art Oliver, running for the U.S. Presidency on the Libertarian ticket, agree with you. Their radical platform includes selling most federal property and using that money to pay off the national debt and privatize Social Security. They would eliminate almost every federal program you can think of (except the military), making the income tax unnecessary.

While most people feel that Browne and Oliver go too far in their platform, anyone who respects our Constitutional freedoms should still vote for them. If elected they would be checked by a Republican- or Democrat-controlled Congress, but even if, in the actual best case scenario, they just take a few million votes, the major parties will start paying attention.

There are plenty of differences between the Democrats and the Republicans, but in the end neither party really understands what a constitutional republic is. The principle behind a constitutional republic is that the minority is protected from the whims of the majority. The majority cannot tax people and spend it to support proselytizing by a favored religious sect; the majority cannot decide how much a person needs to save to retire. The two major candidates will abuse the federal government in different ways, and you probably find one much more desirable than the other, but your vote is still best spent reminding them that people can make better decisions about their life than any bureaucrat.

For some reason, we have a Social Security system in which every American, regardless of whether they need it or not, regardless of whether they could do better on their own or not, has part or all of their retirement handled by bureaucrats. And neither Mr. Gore nor Mr. Bush really plans to change that.

Somehow we have a set of drug laws which allow supposed enforcers to seize property without any court process - and keep them as long as the want. Both parties give out pork to their biggest supporters, be they corporations receiving corporate welfare or unions getting their jobs protected from other countries doing a better job. No wonder so much money gets donated: it's a better investment than the stock market. The best solution anyone in either of the major parties seems to have is limiting how much money a person can donate, which just forces candidates to spend more time fund raising and also happens to limit what people can put on an internet site or newspaper editorial. It's only Browne and Oliver who actually want to fix the problem by making political candidates a less profitable investment.

This election you have three choices: the status quo (with less abortion, a tax cut, more enforcement of gun laws), the status quo (with more environmental protection laws, a different tax cut, and more gun laws which aren't enforced), or Harry Browne and Art Oliver (and end Social Security, the drug war, and the national debt with one vote).

Henry Towsner is a sophomore majoring in Mathematicsal and Computational Science. Henry would be in seventh heaven if a libertarian won the White House.
