RU-486: Stanford's New Drug of Choice
Stanford's Cowell Health Center has leaped into the forefront of political debate by contemplating adding RU-486 to its long list of women's health services. RU-486 induces a surgery-free abortion if used within the first seven weeks of pregnancy, although complications requiring surgery have been documented in 5% to 8% percent of women using the drug. This controversial French drug, which has been widely available in other parts of the world for years, will be produced in America under the brand name "Mifeprex." The drug was approved for production by the FDA on September 28 and will be available by prescription only.

Bush v. Gore: The Saga Continues
The first of three scheduled presidential debates was greeted with a mixture of excitement and apathy on the Farm late Tuesday evening, as was the Vice-Presidential debate on Thursday. Although polling has not revealed a clear "winner," Stanford students tend to favor Vice-President Al Gore, despite his audible sighs, grunts, frowns, and other displays of disappointment at Governor George W. Bush's comments. Both Republicans and Democrats contend that this series of debates will show Bush's and Gore's true character, charm, and plan for the future of America. The candidates are similar in some respects, but offer different solutions to problems of prescription drugs, Medicare, military readiness, and Social Security.

"Dish"ing Out More than Necessary
Stanford's new "Dish" policy has come under attack from conservatives and liberals alike. The administration has closed off all parts of the foothills except a single fenced-in path. A recent Daily editorial claims that the armed guards who vigilantly protect the environment from joggers and dogs are "gratuitous and unnecessary." There is widespread agreement that the environment must be protected from blatant and destructive misuse, but some argue that by placing over fifty regulatory signs, paving access roads, and ordering police patrols, the University has caused as much damage as the bikers and walkers it limits. This unilateral public outcry from both town and gown may force Stanford to rethink its "Dish" policy in the near future.
