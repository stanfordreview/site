The Last Page
READ THIS: SAVE $180!!!
We've decided to save the children.

Despite a lifetime of unyielding allegiance to subverting any semblance of democracy, rationality, and happiness on the Farm, the Review has finally given in to its Maoist underbelly. Resistance is futile when the cause is this spirited.

We want all students to know they can rise up against their oppressors and receive cash back from their university fees, thwarting the narrow-interest ASSU groups.

Here's what you can do: visit assu.stanford.edu. Click for the information about the fee refund. Type in the usual name and student ID. Click "yes" for all the buttons there. Click "submit" and then "yes."

By the end of the quarter, you'll receive a crispy $62 check. Do this for all three quarters and you save $186. Not bad for five minutes work of work, eh?

Each quarter, in addition to charging every student $10,000 for tuition, room, board, and chandeliers in building 30, Stanford assesses a $62 fee used to pay for the activities of and staff development expenses of ASSU groups. If you think that the ASSU can do a better job of distributing your money than you can, feel free to disregard this opportunity. Feel free also then to send the ASSU more of your own money. For $186 per student, you can guarantee that every student has his own special interest group.

But if you're like us, save your money. Teach the ASSU-funded special interest groups to have the courtesy of showing up at your door and taking your money the old-fashioned way.

Innocence Lost - Or - Days Of Orientation
Thursday: Go to sleep absolutely thrilled. Tomorrow begins Stanford! Tomorrow ends this blase world of fetid bourgeoisie--this intellectual charnel house, onerous to the soul and oppressive to the spirit.

Friday: Arrive at Stanford University to blaring hip-hop and senselessly happy RA's. Kiss Mommy, Daddy, Brother, Aunt, Uncle, Boyfriend, Girlfriend, God Mother, Parish Priest, Dog, Cat, Hampster, Car Keys, Cleanliness, Abstinence, and Chastity. Move into Branner. Stick it to The Man by hammering mammoth nails into the walls to support a 90 pound hammer and sickle. Meet roommate, roommate's family, and roommate's incessantly blaring bassoon. Go to CoHo at 2 am; find it's closed.

Saturday: Wake up on couch in Stern lounge. Parents return to say goodbye once more. Eschew mystery meat, dine well in FloMo. Go laugh at parents taking their prospective-Stanford-toddlers to visit campus. Sell soul to buy entire reading list for SLE. Roommate brings in guy/chick/RA/AA/FA/discordant musical instrument/hamster for late night soiree. Abandon English; embrace Stanford's Particularly Labyrinthine Endemic Acronymic Newspeak (SPLEAN).

Sunday: Wake up in Paloma Dining Hall. Go to Flicks. Hit on head with heavy, giant rolled up wad of The Wall Street Journal during paper fight. Write letter to the Daily calling for a ban on such hate-filled, violence-causing publications. Rail against The Stanford Review, Prop. 209, Capitalism, and Flannel Shirts in the same breath. Go to CoHo at 1 am; find it's closed.

Monday: Wake up in bathroom. Check out murals in Casa Zapata, get into a fervent argument with bigot who opposes artistic free expression. Get first copy of The Stanford Review. Immediately burn it.

Tuesday: Wake up in Main Quad. Need something to cover naked self. Use The Stanford Daily. Spend much time pretentiously perusing the art being sold outside the bookstore. Go to the CoHo; find it's closed. Go to the orientation presentation -- Faces Of The Community -- and learn that Stanford is far from diverse: that being pro-diversity means supporting the grape boycott, denying whiteness, and always granting tenure to Native American professors, and that members of racial minorities who are economically successful are "whitewashed."

Wednesday: Wake up in professor's apartment. Follow professor to class. Stop by CoHo; find it's closed. Use words "celeritous" and "paradigm" whenever possible. Repeat other students' comments in more pompous language. Hold breath when passing Hoover Tower. Deride economics as soulless. Visit the Enchanted Broccoli Forest for Enlightenment; hold political discussion with a Giant Nihilistic Mushroom. Return to room for first time in three days. Shower.

Thursday: Wake up with the Giant Mushroom. Learn to conform and cease all independent thought. Get expelled from SLE, but graduate Stanford early with honors. Go to Harvard for grad school.

Alex Robbins is a freshman. He is decidedly not a mushroom.
