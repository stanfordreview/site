The Farm's Political Cash Cows
Each election sees campaign collecting and spending climb to ever higher peaks, and the year 2000 will be no exception. At Stanford and nationwide an outpouring of gifts has fattened campaign bank accounts to record girth. As of October 1, the residents of the 94305 zip code have donated about $313,000 to various political causes, a figure that surpasses the same area's total giving in the 1996 presidential season by twofold and will continue to swell through Election Day. .....Full story in Front Page.....by Michael R. Fainelli

ASSU Sponsors Training Seminar For Radical Activists
Last week, Stanford students, largely unknowingly, funded a two-day seminar for liberal activists. The two-day event, named Workshops on Action for Change, or WAC, was a replacement for the Grassroots Organizing Weekend (GROW) which has taken place on campus over the previous three years. The WAC offered a series of workshops aimed at improving the effectiveness of Stanford activist groups. .....Full story in Front Page.....by Jim Carlson

A Note From Bob
People should be able to choose what kind of speech their money supports. Conversely, people should be able to use their money to support whatever kind of speech they choose. For instance, the ASSU rightly allows students to request back their special fees - that $52 levied on each student to support student organizations. While a large portion of these fees go to community service groups like the Stanford Community Carnival or generally inoffensive organizations like the Chappie, some of the money also goes to political organizations like SEAS - Students for Environmental Action at Stanford - as well as occasionally political organizations like the Black Student Union and the Asian American Students Association......Full story in A Note From Bob

Dinesh D'Souza Speaks: The End Of Affirmitive Action
On October 16th, Dinesh D'Souza, the best-selling author of Illiberal Education and the End of Racism, spoke to a packed auditorium in the Law School about "The End of Affirmative Action." .....Full story in News.....by Ricardo Trigueros and Bob McGrew

A Good Party Never Dies: Reviving The Greens
The Green Party has joined the Republican and Democratic Parties among the parties represented by Stanford students. At a "teach-in" at Synergy on October 13, over 50 students showed up to support the newly re-formed Stanford Greens, who have not had an official presence on campus since 1992. The purpose of the event was to educate the participants about key issues. .....Full story in News.....by Matt Barrett

Indigenous People's Day And The Demons Of Columbus
This year's Columbus Day went hardly noticed by many Cardinals. Aside from postal workers taking a day off, business continued as usual for most of the Stanford community. Stanford's Native American community, however, saw the federal holiday as an opportunity to strengthen their solidarity and celebrate their heritage. .....Full story in News.....by Keun Lee

Don't Throw Your Vote Away: Vote For A Third Party
It is, in the worst possible sense of the word, conventional wisdom that voting for a third party is a "wasted vote." Everyone knows that the only viable candidates are Bush and Gore, and that the only way to influence the election, and therefore to have any meaningful voice in the American democracy, is to vote for a candidate nominated by a major party, holding your nose if necessary. .....Full story in Opinion.....by Henry Towsner
