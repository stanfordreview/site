News Briefs
Take a look at recent events that have happened around campus and the political scene.....compiled by Kurt Berglund

Dinesh D'Souza Speaks: The End Of Affirmitive Action
On October 16th, Dinesh D'Souza, the best-selling author of Illiberal Education and the End of Racism, spoke to a packed auditorium in the Law School about "The End of Affirmative Action." .....Full story.....by Ricardo Trigueros and Bob McGrew

A Good Party Never Dies: Reviving The Greens
The Green Party has joined the Republican and Democratic Parties among the parties represented by Stanford students. At a "teach-in" at Synergy on October 13, over 50 students showed up to support the newly re-formed Stanford Greens, who have not had an official presence on campus since 1992. The purpose of the event was to educate the participants about key issues. .....Full story.....by Matt Barrett

Indigenous People's Day And The Demons Of Columbus
This year's Columbus Day went hardly noticed by many Cardinals. Aside from postal workers taking a day off, business continued as usual for most of the Stanford community. Stanford's Native American community, however, saw the federal holiday as an opportunity to strengthen their solidarity and celebrate their heritage. .....Full story.....by Keun Lee
