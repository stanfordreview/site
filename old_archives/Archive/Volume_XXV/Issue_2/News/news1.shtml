Computer Pioneer Inaugurated as Stanford's 10th President
On Friday, October 20, John Hennessy was inaugurated as the tenth President of Stanford University. Hennessy replaced Gerhard Casper, whose inauguration took place in 1992. Hennessy's stay at the University began in 1977 as an assistant professor of electrical engineering. In 1983 he became an associate professor, and in 1986 he became a full professor of electrical engineering and computer science. Hennessy has been a pioneer in the field of computer architecture. In 1981 he helped to create the RISC computer architecture, which has revolutionized the computer industry. Besides being a professor, he is a co-founder of MIPS Technologies, a manufacturer of computer microprocessors and one of the first companies to implement to the RISC architecture.

Paging the New Dean
A new Dean of the Stanford Medical School may be announced in the coming weeks. A committee led by President John Hennessy and Pathology Department Char Steve Galli has narrowed down a list of hundreds of candidates to twelve people. Candidates were selected based upon recommendations from faculty and from responses to advertisements placed in academic and scholastic journals. Initially, it was hoped that a decision would be reached before the end of Casper's term as President, but delays and questions about the position's responsibilities have led to numerous delays.

White Allies Dialogue Group
A group of Stanford students is setting up a "White Allies Dialogue Group" for those white males who understand that they are irredeemably privileged and racist. If you don't yet realize that you are racist, however, please don't apply. "Do you identify yourself as white? Do you identify yourself as male? In this year-long dialogue, we examine in depth the harmful effects of white privilege and the responsibilities and challenges of being white and wanting to confront racism in yourself and your world. ...We encourage participants to have already formed an impression of their own white privilege and truly desire to educate and challenge themselves." [from the e-mail forward advertising the group.]

Land for Rent
Stanford President John Hennessy and Palo Alto Mayor Liz Kniss recently announced a plan to rent six acres of University land at the rate of $1 per year for 51 years to Palo Alto for the construction of "a non-profit community center." The city will use the land to relocate the Jewish Community Center, which will allow for the construction of a much-needed middle school at the JCC current location.
