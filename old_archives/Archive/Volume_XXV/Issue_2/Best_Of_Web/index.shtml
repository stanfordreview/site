Best Of the Web
Best Nonpartisan Political Site: www.slate.com

Slate, Microsoft's foray into online publishing, is the best political analysis site on the web. Ulike NRO, which we profiled last issue, Slate purports to be non-partisan and actually does a pretty good job. They attempt to take advantage of the new style of the web, structuring many of their articles as conversations. They offer constantly updated pages and instant analysis of important political events. Be sure to check out kausfiles, written by Mickey Kaus, a liberal but a particularly clear-eyed one. For political analysis from a generally balanced perspective, Slate.com is definitely your best choice.

Runner-up: www.stanfordreview.org
So we're no Slate either. And we do have a bit of bias. But, on the Stanford campus, we're your best choice. In fact, we're your only choice. Accessible anytime, anywhere, stanfordreview.org will offer you the latest conservative wisdom and "instant" political analysis from some of Stanford's best analysts. Check us out!
