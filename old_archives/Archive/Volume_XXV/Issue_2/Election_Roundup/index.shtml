Election Roundup
With the third and final Presidential debate still fresh in Americans' minds, Texas Governor George W. Bush seems to be enjoying a slender lead in recent polls. Released soon after the latest nationally televised political contest, an October 18th - 20th Gallup survey of likely voters indicated that Vice-President Gore might not have performed as well on stage as his campaign advisors hoped he would. According to the nationwide poll, Gore is lagging behind the Texas Governor by 11 percentage points. While Bush's 51% to 40% lead over the Vice-President is admittedly only three points beyond both sides of the 4% margin of error, this boon to the Texas governor's campaign appears to have come despite the high expectations surrounding his opponent's debating proficiency. Political pundits interpret the latest figures to suggest that voters are more closely focused on the issues of personality and character than on governmental policy. However, with Bush only recently pulling ahead among the fickle, independent swing voters, it is still impossible to determine in which direction the scales will tip come election day.
