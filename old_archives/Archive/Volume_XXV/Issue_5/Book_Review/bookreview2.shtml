Book Review
Note To Socialists: Learn To Get Along
It Didn't Happen Here: Why Socialism Failed in the United States By Seymore Martin Lipset and Gary Marks

Primarily because they were poor politicians is the answer Seymour Martin Lipset and Gary Marks offer to the question posed by their book It Didn't Happen Here: Why Socialism Failed in the United States.

They begin by examining most of the common explanations given for why America is the only major democracy where some sort of labor or social democratic party is not a major force. The examine the plurality system of electing a president, the weakness of third parties in the U.S. in general, the high number of immigrants, the split of power between the federal and state governments, and others. In each case they compare the American situation with that in other countries and look at how that factor affected the development of a socialist party.

The analyses are detailed at the beginning of the book, and become extremely quantitative by the end. This is not a book for light reading: in proving their points, Messrs. Lipset and Marks delve deep into the history, examining voting patterns in individual states and cities around the United States and much of Europe, Canada, and Australia. They dismiss many supposed explanations for the lack of socialist development in America by finding examples of other countries where the same conditions prevailed and socialism still became quite strong, sometimes with the apparent help of the factor thought to hinder it. Other suggestions the show to be inconsistent by linking those factors to high points in socialism or by giving examples, some within the US, of where that trait was turned to an advantage by socialists.

Throughout the book, Messrs. Lipset and Marks slowly advance the evidence for their own theory. American socialists were far more ideologically "pure" than their foreign counterparts. They refused to work with the unions or more moderate groups, insisting on maintaining their platform in full without compromise. When they did manage to get people elected, they often ousted them or forced them to resign when those officials were forced to work with other elected officers. A typical example was a city councilor (the only on that city council) who voted for a non-socialist to be city council chair. On the occasions when some socialists did work within other parties, other socialists failed to support them and even opposed them. For example the Non-Partisan League, which gained control over the Republican party in first one and then several states, nominating and getting elected several governors, senators, and other positions. Rather than mimicking this success, other socialist groups criticized the League, not even backing its candidates.

Because they never managed to work together with the unions as socialists in other countries did (although at various times socialists controlled a number of different unions, they never gained a long-term influence over either the AFL or the CIO), American socialists never acquired substantial support from the working class. Indeed, much of the base of socialist support was from intellectuals rather than the laborers who socialists purported to benefit. The lack of a groundswell of support was yet another nail in the socialist coffin in the United States.

It Didn't Happen Here is a dense, sometimes tedious book, but for good reason. Messrs. Lipset and Marks make use of the details to argue their point decisively, never straying from what they can support with example after example. The book is more suited to those studying history seriously than to casual reading, but even the casual reader may find it worthwhile to read it prepared to skim when appropriate. If nothing else, this book provides a huge amount of information about the history of American political parties for the last century, and that alone makes the book interesting enough to be worth reading.

Henry Towsner is a sophomore majoring Mathematical and Computational Science. He was pleased to see yet another socialist candidate fail this election.
