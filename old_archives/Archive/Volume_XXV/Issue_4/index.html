Students Rally To Protest Dioxin
Nearly 150 Stanford students gathered at White Plaza on November 1st to protest the "environmental racism" of the Stanford Hospital and Medical Center. Organized by Students for Environmental Action at Stanford (SEAS), the hour-long student rally expressed outrage through the clanging of pots and pans, a twelve-foot puppet, and the Radical Cheerleaders. .....Full story.....by Grey Baker

A Interview With Provost John Etchemendy
TSR: Why did you accept the offer to be provost?

Etchemendy: That's what I keep asking myself. I never had plans to be part of the university administration. I don't see this as a step in a career. But I think the reason I agreed to it was largely because of the year I spent on the presidential search committee. One of the things that becomes more apparent to you is just how much you care about the university.

She saw society's problems, and identified ways to make things better. It's Time To End The Boycott
We are on the eve of the annual grape boycott. Since 1988 and more so since1994, when four students staged a hunger strike to show their support for the United Farm Workers grape boycott, Stanford students have voted every year on whether their dining hall should serve grapes. It is time for the administration to reform its policy and cancel the vote. Students who wish to participate in the boycott should be able to do so, but students who wish to separate themselves from the issue should also be able to do so. .....Full story.....by Matthew Barrett
