Best Of the Web
Best Libertarian Site: reason.com

"Free Minds and Free Markets" has been Reason magazine's slogan since 1968, when it was founded as a student publication at Boston University. It has since matured into a magazine devoted to ideas advancing individual liberty - not just traditional libertarian themes like privatization and property rights but venturing as well into culture and politics. As intellectual publications go, Reason Online is one of the best web versions of bound publications. The site fronts the full text of the last issue as well as the articles for the next, published as they are submitted. Often two or three articles pop up daily. For intellectual content from a libertarian perspective, Reason Online can't be beat.

Runner-up: www.stanfordreview.org
Every week, the Review is out there defending students' liberties - taking a stand on the speech code, informing students where their special fees go, and defending every student's right to choose grapes. And what great libertarian conservative newspaper would be complete without a web site? Our web page features the full content of every issue as well as the full versions of our interviews. Check it out!
