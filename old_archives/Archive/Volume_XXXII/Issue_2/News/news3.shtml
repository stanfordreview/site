Wonder Where Your Hard-Earned Money is Going?
by Christine Boehm
News Editor

The Review recently learned that the tuition hike announced last week is needed for some very important projects. We hope to make it a little less painful for you to work longer hours, take out bigger loans, or ask daddy for a bigger check by showing you exactly where your tuition money is going.

In order to protect students from the devastating emotional impact of hitting and killing a tiger salamander, the Board of Trustees has approved the construction of more tiger salamander crossings. It is also asking the entire faculty of the School of Education to spend the month of March teaching the amphibians how to use the crossings. Stanford students in education classes will be taught by the substitute teachers of the local public schools.


 Taking its cue from V-week, it occured to the Board of Trustees that the Stanford community may not know that other serious crimes are not permitted on the Farm. The Board of Trustees has approved the purchase of miles of red tape to let people know that Stanford is also a murder, assault and robbery-free zone.
The most complicated project is sure to pull on everyone's heartstrings. The Board of Trustees is funding an interdisciplinary study by the surgery and art deparments in plastic surgery for sculptures. It is hoped that the study will find a way to correct the Claw so that the other fountains will stop making fun of his deformity. Barring that, ResEd will hold a crossing the line session to make all fountains on campus more sensitive of differences. Determined to have Stanford ranked number one by US News and World Report, the Board of Trustees has allocated several million dollars to run real coffee through the fountain outside of Green Library. The plan is to make Stanford students the most productive in the nation by alleviating their need to sleep.
