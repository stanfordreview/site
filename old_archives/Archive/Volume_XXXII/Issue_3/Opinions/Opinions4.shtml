Gay Rights Movement Has Not Reached Moral Maturity
by Chris Fish

The Review’s position is neither anti-gay nor anti-lesbian. Although we will unquestionably be accused of all forms of bigotry and hate mongering for this issue, The Review does not contend that theres anything immoral about being a homosexual. Nor does The Review’s stance in opposition of gay marriage have anything to do with hatred or a mean spirit. This issue is about one simple fact: marriage is between a man and a woman. The family is the most basic unit of human civilization and to undermine it is to potentially undermine the bedrock that we have built our beautiful cities and lives upon.

If we as a society are to tear out a piece of the basic familial fabric that has defined us since the earliest human times, we must ask ourselves what is to replace it. Has the gay rights movement earned the maturity to warrant becoming a pillar of our civilization? The flamboyant homosexuals of the San Francisco gay pride parades appear more like a group of drunken fraternity boys coated in body paint and makeup than a dignified minority group arguing that it has the inherent moral authority to warrant a fundamental change to the structure of our society.

Rewriting the rules of the world is no task to be taken lightly. Man has always married woman. This basic rule may need amending yet, but when the civil disobedience of the homosexual community is marked not with the innate dignity of Ghandi but rather that of Howard Stern, has that time come yet?

Those who are in favor of gay marriage have and will continue to present this debate as a battle between the bigots and the hate mongers on one side and the progressive and open-minded on the other. But this is nothing more than utter deception and intimidation.

This is a question of do we or do we not want to amend the underpinnings that have defined civilized society. I ask all of you, in considering your support for gay marriage, to look inward and ask yourself how you believe society should operate. If you feel that it is a time for change and time to grant a new privilege to a group then by all means pursue it vigorously through the appropriate legal channels. But if when you think family you envision two children in the loving of embrace of a mother and father, then don’t be afraid to stand up and say marriage is only between a man and a woman. You are neither a hateful person nor close-minded for feeling this way.

I only hope in the end that those who stand up and support gay marriage do it for the right reasons and realize the full scale implications of their actions.
