Horowitz Slams Leftist Academia

by Nick Hoy
Investigative News Editor

 

Respected author and political commentator David Horowitz attacked the “liberal” leftist indoctrination of students at universities across the nation in a well-attended talk at Kresge Auditorium on February 1st.

Displaying his renowned sharp wit and keen understanding of the fragile power politics between left and right, Horowitz informed a shocked and increasingly angered audience of the “totalitarian” mentality of established leftist academia, one that compromised quality and balance of education in order to advance their own personal political agendas.

“You can’t get a good education if they’re only telling you half the story,” Horowitz said.

And Horowitz does indeed present compelling evidence that we are being treated to only the leftist interpretation of politics, history, science, and religion. According to research conducted by Horowitz at Stanford and Berkeley: “among junior professors, the ratio of political leftists outnumbered conservatives and republicans by a factor of 30:1.”

The cause of this astonishing ratio, according to Horowitz, is the blanket institution of a hiring blacklist, which in turn has transformed the entire culture of America’s universities into one hostile to “intellectual diversity."

Horowitz recalled being invited to the University of Delaware by the campus’ chapter of the College Republicans.
The group’s faculty advisor, a respected professor, told Horowitz that “he hadn’t been allowed to be on a [staff] search committee since 1985.” On that last occasion, over fifteen years past, the faculty advisor had “hired a Marxist.” He was most qualified, and “when you’re a conservative, you believe in the process.”

The blacklisting is at this point so institutionalized, said Horowitz, that “if you are a terrorist, or a supporter of terrorists, you can be invited by the faculty of Hamilton [College]… to work as a visiting professor. But not so if you are a conservative.” The terrorist in question is Susan Rosenberg, who was convicted in 1984 to a 58-year prison sentence for the “possession of over 700 pounds of explosives and a stockpile of illicit weapons.”

Horowitz believes that the leftists are by nature “missionaries,” convinced that if they successfully indoctrinate society with their “liberalism” and gain power, they will be able to rid America of all of its social ills. But “there’s nothing
liberal about someone who can’t tolerate a dissenting view,” Horowitz noted.

Horowitz himself has personal experience that attests to the importance of hearing both sides of the story. Raised by Marxist parents, he received a graduate degree from Berkeley in 1961 and soon became an influential figure in the New Left movement.

But disillusioned with the ultra-violent tactics of the Black Panthers and the destructive Marxism of the New Left, Horowitz founded the influential conservative think-tank Center for the Study of Popular Culture, in 1988. Horowitz’s political evolution was chronicled in his autobiography Radical Son: A Generational Odyssey, described by author George Gilder as “the first great American autobiography of his generation.”

Thus Horowitz is particularly concerned that American students are not being offered the same opportunities to chart their own intellectual course, and potentially arrive at a similar evolution.

“The intellectual level at American universities is lower than it has ever been in my lifetime,” Horowitz said.
