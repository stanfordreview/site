 The Iraq War: Worth the Cost? Pro
 

Sometimes being wealthy is complicated. Just ask Stanford University. The January 18th ASSU Senate bill, “To Ensure Endowment Investment Responsibility,” was passed with a 9-3 majority (with two abstentions), the bare minimum required. It was written and sponsored by ASSU Senator and Chair of the Senate Advocacy Committee Omar Shakir, based on presentations made to the Senate by The Stanford Coalition for Investment Disclosure (SCID) and Linda Kimball of the Stanford Management Company.

Proponents of the Iraq War often qualify their position with a tacit admission that the evidence needed to justify the liberation of Iraq did not pan out. President Bush, the argument runs, made the best decision based on the best intelligence available. While this approach has its merits, hindsight highlights not only the mistakes; it also strengthens the case for invasion in the context of the Global War on Terrorism. After September 11th, the United States essentially declared war on all international terrorist groups and the regimes that sponsor them. Any nation that supports these groups, then, is a legitimate target.

Physician-Assisted Suicide Scrutinized The Iraq War: Worth the Cost? Con

On Tuesday, February 22, the Supreme Court agreed to hear an interesting new case on physician-assisted suicide. The plaintiff, contrary to what we might expect, isn’t a doctor (or death-eager citizen) claiming a state law prohibiting the practice violates his constitutional rights. The Court, in fact, has already rejected the argument that the Constitution affords terminally ill people a “right to die.”

“A government that is big enough to give you everything you want is big enough to take it all away.” Barry Goldwater’s words ring as true on the 2nd anniversary of the invasion of Iraq as they were when he first uttered them. Unfortunately, President George W. Bush seems unconcerned that a government big enough to bring freedom and democracy to the rest of the world is certainly big enough to curtail it at home.
