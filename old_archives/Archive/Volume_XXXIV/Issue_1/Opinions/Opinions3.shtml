Racial Profiling: Necessary Evil? Pro

by Jack Greer
Staff Writer

There is one good reason why racial profiling should be acceptable: to help stop terrorists. Whether we like it or not, the United States is engaged in a global struggle against Islamic fundamentalists. Most of these terrorists hail from Arab countries. Therefore, it makes sense to pay special attention to those of Arab descent in order to deter and disrupt future terrorist attacks.

A cursory look over the nineteen hijackers on 9/11 helps illustrate this point: One was Lebanese. One was Egyptian. Two were from the United Arab Emirates. Fifteen were from Saudi Arabia. All Arabs, all Muslims. In fact, the FAA’s threat-profiling system, known as CAPPS, correctly identified ten of the nineteen prior to boarding their flights. CAPPS is partly based on the passenger’s travel history (I say partly because the actual algorithm is classified), so we can safely infer that all those Arab nationals were fingered because they traveled from the Middle East—and let’s face it, most people flying in from Arab countries are Arabs.

In a two-year-old article in the New York Post, terrorism expert Daniel Pipes noted: “No other enemy has remotely the same record” as Islamic fundamentalists. Since 9/11, they have struck in Algeria, Jordan, Kuwait, India, Indonesia, Iraq, Israel, Lebanon, Morocco, the Netherlands, the Philippines, Russia, Saudi Arabia, Spain, Thailand, Tunisia, and Turkey. Muslim terror cells have been uncovered in Belgium, Britain, France, Germany, Italy, Singapore, and Yemen—and the United States. Needless to say, most of these Islamic terrorists were Arabs.If the majority of those who seek to destroy us are Arabs, and if one of their traditional methods of attack has been to use airliners in some capacity (be it suicide or hostage-taking), is there any good reason not to focus a certain degree of energy on this group of people?

This is not to say that racial profiling can in any way be viewed as a panacea as many of America’s enemies in the Global War on Terrorism have not been Arabs. Some notable examples spring to mind: John Walker Lindh, Lee Boyd Malvo and John Allen Muhammad, and Richard Reid. Yet these examples are notable because they are exceptional. Osama bin Laden, Ayman al-Zawahiri, Abu Musab al-Zarqawi, Sheikh Abdel Rahman, and Ramzi Yousef are all Arab.

The United States must employ its vast array of counter- terrorism tools to stop terrorists of all racial backgrounds. We should humbly admit that we will not catch them all and likewise be ready to make mistakes. As John Derbyshire wrote in National Review, our nation should “treat everyone with courtesy and apologize to the inconvenienced innocent.”

The system will be an inconvenience to some, but through it we can save many more.In the words of Oubai Mohammad Shahbandar, a Muslim American writing on FrontPageMag.com, “Without life, there can be no liberty and pursuit of happiness. I pray the government will continue to take all steps necessary to safeguard us all. Mr. President, profile the terrorists.”
