European Leftist Environmentalists Seem Reac­tionary, Not Intellectual

Environmental consultant Shellen­berger urges leftist students to form a ‘flourishing intellectual culture,’ while at the same event, his pal and ex-Sierra Club President Werbach tells students to ‘disagree violently with Baby Boomer professors, even with no reason to.” Perhaps their definition of intellectualism differs from mine, but irrational and violent disagreement is reactionary, not intellectual. It’s time people realize (especially at Stanford) that reactionary behavior comes from both the extreme left and extreme right in fairly equal doses.

Blake Masters
Freshman, Economics and Poli Sci

‘IHUM is crap’

I’m impressed you guys printed my letter [“Infuriated Student Rails Against Review’s Special Election Edi­tion (April 4, 2005)”]. The title you put on it is a bit silly but what can you do? On an interesting note, I actually agree with you guys this time about IHUM. IHUM is crap. Writing two papers a quarter does not improve your argu­mentative skill. I put more work into one PWR paper (not even my big one) than I do into both IHUM papers. I wrote more papers in a high school English class than in IHUM. And as for commonality between classes and a classical education, I’m with you there, too. I took the IHUM class that read Machiavelli, Locke, Plato, etc. because I knew I wouldn’t get that elsewhere. It’s good stuff for most people to have at least some familiarity with, even though I didn’t really enjoy reading it. There is hardly anything in common between IHUM classes. One of them is almost entirely about South Africa , while the one I’m in now has been an overview of the history of Western philosophy, from the existence of God to the existence of knowledge. I am usually in favor of giving students lots of choice, but IHUM should be more standard if we all have to take it.

Joel Cretan
Freshman, Undeclared
