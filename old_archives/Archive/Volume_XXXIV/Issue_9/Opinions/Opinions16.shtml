Why Blame America? America Isn't Evil

by Jack Greer
Staff Writer

There exists a tendency among academics and pseudo-intellectuals to blame America for all the ills of the world. Unfortunately, many of them live on this campus. This is a disease, an incredibly shaky logical framework that is founded on little fact and much mindless idiocy. Got a problem? Blame the United States.

I confess I’m by no means the first to advance this proposition. After all, former U.S. ambassador to the United Nations (an organization rarely blamed for anything by those who regularly criticize its greatest source of funding) Jeane Kirkpatrick berated “the San Francisco Democrats” way back in 1984. From the Sandinistas to Grenada, from Lebanon to arms control, Kirkpatrick defended President Reagan’s policies and attacked his opponents: “They blamed the United States. But then, they always blame America first.”

It starts at a young age, and I admit that I was once infected by this vile insanity. It was evil Americans who stole the Indians’ land, I was taught; evil Americans who stole Mexicans’ land; evil Americans who waste so much of this planet’s natural resources; evil Americans who waited years before stopping the Holocaust. Some, or all, of these points may indeed be valid, but the constant refrain of how evil America is drags on and on and on. Fortunately, those of us instilled at a young age with a love for this country can overcome the poison, and those of us who know how to read can confront many of the myths propagated by the Blame America First crowd.

Hollywood and pop culture tell us that the evil CIA is all-powerful and can install dictators wherever it pleases, stooges for evil American presidents to protect or promote their own business interests. How this conspiratorial spy agency failed to topple Saddam and secure oil for evil America in the 1990s doesn’t really seem to matter—after all, Saddam was a CIA agent. I’m not kidding. Two Structured Liberal Education students actually told me that. They also said Osama bin Laden was trained by the CIA, despite the fact that the CIA supported the tens upon tens of thousands of indigenous Afghan rebels more than foreign fighters from the Gulf.

This all goes back to the Cold War, of course. On September 11, 1973 (a date, no doubt, which links the CIA to 9/11), Chilean President Salvador Allende died in a coup. Controversy exists over whether or not he killed himself, but the fact remains that the coup was instigated by several Chilean generals, not the CIA. But no, the constant refrain we hear is that the CIA installed Pinochet—and countless other dictators, only a few of which anyone can name. These are secret dictators, you see.

America is also to blame for both theaters of World War II. It was selfish America who stayed out of the League of Nations, after all—another spineless international body, paralyzed as Japan invaded China, Bolivia and Paraguay went to war, Italy invaded Ethiopia, Spain erupted in civil war, Germany rearmed, and Poland and Lithuania fought over Vilnius. Surely, if only America hadn’t been so arrogant, Hitler could’ve been stopped! Also, if greedy America hadn’t cut-off peaceful Japan’s oil supply, then they wouldn’t have attacked Pearl Harbor. Screw the Manchurians, right?

I could go on, but I’m running out of words. I abhor this disease and don’t think highly of those who harbor the disease, but who am I to judge, right? Well, by golly, I’m an American.
