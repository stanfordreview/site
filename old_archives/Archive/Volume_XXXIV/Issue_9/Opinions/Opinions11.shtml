US Military Deserves Praise for Global Humanitarian Efforts

by Jake Suyderhoud
Deputy Editor

On the 26th of December, disaster struck many of Southeast Asian shores. A magnitude 9 earthquake rocked the ocean, creating a massive tsunami that devastated infrastructure, houses, and lives. The countries that were struck were unable to cope with the travesty and desperately needed aid to prevent further losses. The first response was the United States military. The United States Navy, with vessels deployed in all corners of the world was able to react quickly and get to the areas that needed help the most. The Navy helped to reduce the chance for disease and other infections, helping to prevent many more deaths from the after-effects of the tsunami.

The United States dispatched an aircraft carrier, the USS Abraham Lincoln immediately as well as a Marine Expeditionary Unit to assist in the relief efforts. These units were able do a lot, most notably generate clean, drinkable water for those places whose water sources had become contaminated. This, however, was only the start. By January 12, 15,000 U.S. military personnel, 25 ships and 94 aircraft were participating in the effort. In less then three weeks, the military had delivered 2.2 million pounds of relief supplies, to the affected region.

Using its ships, planes, and helicopters, the military was rapidly able to establish a supply chain to the most stricken areas that were unreachable by other efforts. The ability to respond to disasters is one of the multi-functional military’s best attributes. The military was happy to do this duty, despite the nearly $5 million per day cost to the U.S.

This, however, is just one of many examples of humanitarian aid that the U.S. military provides. The U.S. military is in a distinctive position to help, and it consistently does so. Many forget that the U.S. military is more than a fighting force. Although it is the best in the world in defense, it also has a distinct role to aid those struck by disaster, whether natural or political. To often people forget this aspect of the military. Some, when they see the fighting side, forget the human side of not only the military, but of the whole of American foreign policy.

The fact that America’s soldiers and sailors were happy to do their duty, putting off home a few extra weeks, is something that we all should be proud of. Their efforts saved lives and displayed to some of those hesitant to embrace American foreign policy the true spirit of America.
