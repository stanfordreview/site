Hey Liberals: Give Me My Word Back!

 

I’ll admit it. I spend a fair amount of time clicking around on thefacebook.com. In my perusing, I recently ran across a group of self pro­claimed “very liberals.” It turns out that there are nearly 800 “very liberals” at Stanford. I could hardly believe it. Now, I had always thought that my political views were in the minority here. Never before had I imagined that so many people might share my commitment to individual liberties and rights. I suppose my hopes were a bit naive, however. It seems that liberalism has taken on a new connotation in the past few years.

You see, these days, “liberal” is the hip new thing. Sorry pops, this isn’t the liberalism of yesterday. Those intellectual lightweights like Locke and Mill have nothing on the brilliance of Karl Marx or, if you’re really “with it,” Howard Zinn. What does liberalism mean to a super-hip-cool-rad 18 year old in America? Well, there are a few prerequisites before you can be as cool as Michael Moore.

In general, to be a hip-liberal, one must posses at least three articles of clothing and/or posters idolizing Che Guevara. When possible, sentences containing “the revolution” get bonus cookies. Indeed, a certain “very liberal” friend of mine recently explained to me that Che Guevara represents the idealism central to modern liberalism. And after all, Guevara had a vision to “change the world” as my nameless classmate put it. And boy, did he ever. Guevara got off to a rip roarin’ start by executing anyone who disagreed with him. Yep, nothing like liberal compassion. Then, he decided that in order to establish a society in line with his liberal values, he would befriend Fidel Castro in forming a totalitarian dictatorship. Hmm.

That brings me to my next point. To be a hip-liberal you have to complain about the government non-stop. Yeah, I know, that whole most-prosperous-country-in-the-world thing is kinda sorta accurate, but you’re young angry and rebellious. This is “the revolution” after all. Sure, capitalism has produced more economic growth faster than the world has ever seen, but capitalism also produces corporations… and liberals hate corporations. Yes, evil, big, money generating corporations. The same corporations that most Stanford students will end up working for. Whoops.

Of course, this brings me back to the central tenant of liberalism: liberty. Cool-hip liberals have their own postmodernist spin on liberty too. You see, liberals are all in favor of individual liberty except when it relates to free market exchanges… or the right to bear arms… or school choice… or retirement accounts… or education policy… or health care… ahem… yeah.

In order to escape these apparent contradictions, liberals have decided to tinker with terminology as well. The cool-hip-new term for liberals is “progressive.” It just seems to roll off the tongue better. Kind of like “Miller Lite”… same great taste but half the calories! So when liberal leaders on campus were deciding what to title their upcoming gathering, I’m sure they sat around for hours until finally coming up with the sort of alliteration they were going for… “liberal pot­luck”… hmm no… “socialist potluck”… hmm no… “progressive potluck.” YES! I can’t help but picture the bongo-playing free-love-power-flower hippie proclaiming “naw man, I’m not liberal, I’m PROGRESSIVE.” Yep, progress­ing towards the socialist police state, but whatever dude… its cool, so long as we’re in touch with mother earth’s cosmic positivity rays.

To be honest, I think I’m starting to realize more and more that ivory tower college “progressivism” is as much a fad as it is a failed ideology. Perhaps, then, there is hope that the term “liberal” might be returned to its rightful owners in the future. After all, fads come and go like Pauly Shore’s career. Until then, to my fellow Gen Yers… keep it real… and most of all… stay hip-cool liberal.
