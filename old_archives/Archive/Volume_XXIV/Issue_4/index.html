Environmentalist David Brower Speaks In Front Of Stanford Crowd
On Tuesday, April 18, 3-time Nobel Peace Prize nominee David Brower raised concerns about the environment and financial inequality in front of a scattered audience in Kresge auditorium. The event was part of Earth Week 2000. Addressing the audience of Stanford students, he illustrated the urgent need to make some serious changes in the way mankind approaches the environment.....Full Story in Front Page.....by David Myszewski

Becoming Disillusioned with The Disillusionment: Protest Hoping In The 21st Century
I cannot claim to be a seasoned protester. I was not at Seattle. I have never been jailed for blocking traffic or smashing plate glass windows. I have never marched down the a street holding a paper sign with the words "ban", "free", or "outlaw". My chance to join the ranks of the disillusioned came on April 17 when there was a planned protest in San Francisco against the IMF and World Bank, a mini-Seattle so to speak.....Full Story in Editor's Note.....by Johnny Openshaw

An Interview With Tod Lindberg, Editor Of Policy Review
The Stanford Review: In March, John McCain announced that he was suspending his campaign for President. What to do you think McCain's next step is? Do you think a third party run is viable for him or do you think he will eventually endorse George W. Bush? Tod Lindberg: I do not think that a third party run is viable for him. I think that it would mark the campaign as eccentric more or less from the beginning.....Full Story in Feature.....by Michael New
