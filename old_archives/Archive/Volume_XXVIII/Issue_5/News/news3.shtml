A Cappella Spring Concerts Underway
by Ming Zhu and Hillary Connell
Staff Writers

May is here, and with it comes the yearly spring shows put on by all our campus a cappella groups. From Christian to R&B, rock to country, every musical taste is covered among the eight groups' programs. Here's the low-down to help you decide which you would enjoy the most. Although, as Mixed Company's Alissa Barnett warns, "You'll be missing out if you only see 1!"

Mixed Company
They'll still be wearing their signature red, white, and black, but according to Mixed Co.'s director Alissa Barnett, the spring show will be full of "traditions as well as spontaneity." Besides their trademark "rip-off medley" comprised of songs from the other Stanford a cappella groups, the show will also feature songs from their latest CD, which will be available after the show.
May 10th, 8 PM, Kresge Auditorium, $5

Fleet Street Singers
Fleet Street's spring performance will be centered on the theme "Fleet Street through Time," and will feature everything from pop to jazz and even a little classical. The audience can expect the show's vintage musical assortment to be livened up with the Singers' distinctive sense of humor. As musical director Anthony Laretto mentioned, "Come to Fleet Street for a total-entertainment package."
May 10th, 8 PM, Kresge Auditorium,$5

Talisman
As described by their musical director, Talisman's spring show will be a combination of story-telling, dramatic interpretation, and literary passage readings along with their songs -- creating a "tapestry" of art and music. Their show will include guest performances from Stanford Taiko, the Steppers, and the Gospel Choir -- something for every artistic taste. And of course, there will be those rich voices and powerful melodies too.
May 29th, 8 PM, Dinkelspiel Auditorium , $5 ($3 w/ SUID)

Harmonics
"A cappella on-the-edge." After touring the East Coast and singing at American University, Times Square, and various elementary schools, the Harmonics will be showcasing their eclectic musical talents in a spring show based loosely on a soap opera format. Their spring show will feature songs from their upcoming CD to be released in May of 2003.
May 17th, 8 PM, Kresge Auditorium, $5 ($3 with SUID)

Stanford Mendicants
Musical director Ian McPherson describes the group as a bunch of "musically inclined guys who have a good sense of how to take notes and make them into music." This year, the group traveled to Hong Kong during spring break and returned to start work on their CD, which will be released next spring. For their spring show, we can expect those catchy old favorites that have us humming on the way home.
May 24th, 7:30 PM, Kresge Auditorium, $3

Testimony
Stanford's only Christian a capella group promises "a personal testimony" alongside entertaining skits and a unique musical repertoire with songs from their recently-recorded CD, Echoes. Musical director Steven Ngai offers up "cheap entertainment in the form of three blasphemous skits that will set the Christian religion back centuries as well as great music that goes beyond the cheap entertainment of the skits."
May 4th, 9 PM, Manzanita Dining Hall, Free Admission

Everyday People
EP returns to the Stanford campus after traveling extensively throughout the last year to Cancun, New York, and many places in between. Students can look forward to their tight harmonies and smooth dance numbers as well as their collection of R&B songs. As quoted by their spring show coordinator "the ambiance will be unlike anything else you've ever seen."

May 18th, 8PM, Place: TBA, Free Admission

The a cappella group Counterpoint will be performing their spring show on May 11. They recently finished their latest CD, Cover Charge.
Counterpoint
Having just finished recording their newest CD, Cover Charge, Counterpoint promises that their show, like their CD, will be a diverse mix that will have something for everyone. As put by group member Lindsay Kibuyaga, "we really pull rabbits out of hats and do things that no one would expect." They've planned a step sequence, several video clips, hilarious skits, and of course -- a great night of music.
May 11th, 7:30 PM , Annenberg Auditorium , $4 ($2 w/ SUID)
