Stanford Environmental Law Society Hosts Conference on Natural Resource Disputes
On Saturday April 6, 2002 the Environmental Law Society hosted a conference focusing on specific conflicts in natural resource law which served as example of the larger issues faced in natural resource policy and law. In two panels, one focusing on the Placer County planning process and the other focusing on the recent Klamath Water crisis, panelists from a variety of backgrounds shared their views on approaches to solve these environmental and natural resource problems. The conference ended with dinner and a speech by the Speaker pro Tem of the California State Assembly, who discussed the issues and causes surrounding the recent energy crisis and the possible implications of the bankruptcy reorganization of PG&E on the operation of hydroelectric projects throughout the Sierra Nevada. ....Full story.....by Justin Diener

Feminist Gloria Steinem Speaks at Stanford
Last Thursday, Gloria Steinem, a leading feminist and political activist delivered a speech entitled "Everything you've ever wanted to know about feminism but were afraid to ask" to an overflowing audience at Kresge Auditorium. Ms. Steinem, now 66 and recently married, opened her speech with a solemn promise to the audience: "If all goes well each of us will leave here with [among other things] a new subversive organizing tactic." ....Full story.....by Ryan Wisnesky

Students Organize US-Russia Student Leadership Summit
From April 5 - 12, Stanford hosted the third annual U.S.-Russia Student Leadership Summit that brought together some of the best and brightest college students from the United States and Russia. Together, 25 Russian students and 15 American students gathered to listen to respected authorities in the field of U.S.-Russian relations and discuss current issues with them. ....Full story.....by Mark Zavislak

Student Group Demands Intelligent University Response to Labor Issues
There have been many labor disputes this year on the Farm, and more than one group has been formed regarding different parts of the labor issues. Pat Bomhack has recently created the campaign for Stanford to join the Workers Rights Consortium (WRC). "We are going to meet with [President] Hennessey next Wednesday [April 18th], present our concerns, and present proposals that will include but are not limited to joining the WRC," he said in a phone interview. ....Full story.....by Joe Lonsdale
