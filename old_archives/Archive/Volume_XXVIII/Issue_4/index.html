Stanford Environmental Law Society Hosts Conference on Natural Resource Disputes
On Saturday April 6, 2002 the Environmental Law Society hosted a conference focusing on specific conflicts in natural resource law which served as example of the larger issues faced in natural resource policy and law. In two panels, one focusing on the Placer County planning process and the other focusing on the recent Klamath Water crisis, panelists from a variety of backgrounds shared their views on approaches to solve these environmental and natural resource problems. The conference ended with dinner and a speech by the Speaker pro Tem of the California State Assembly, who discussed the issues and causes surrounding the recent energy crisis and the possible implications of the bankruptcy reorganization of PG&E on the operation of hydroelectric projects throughout the Sierra Nevada. ....Full story in News.....by Justin Diener

Giving "Diverse" Students Extra Financial Aid Is Worse than Nothing
The racial and socioeconomic realities of our society result in minority students needing financial aid more often than their Caucasian and Asian counterparts. This is not Stanford's doing, and the disparity here is nothing that Stanford can fix. What is wrong, however, is when two people of similar economic but different racial backgrounds are given different amounts of monetary aid. This policy is racist, divisive, and harmful to all parties involved. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Joe Lonsdale

Financial Aid Office Should Present Pro-Diversity Policy with Pride
The financial aid office has a policy of reducing the self-help expectation for students who "bring diversity to campus", meaning that they are of some particular ethnic group (see the news article for more information). The self-help portion of the total cost of education here is minuscule to begin with in comparison to the tuition, room and board (though it's certainly not negligible in comparison to something cheap, like a new car). So let's assume that for a black or Hispanic student, the self-help portion is reduced by $1200--this is based on the 1995 figure with a bit tacked on for inflation and leeway, because the Financial Aid Office declined to give any specifics. Even though this does not amount to large-scale redistribution of wealth, we are obliged to question the motives of anyone implementing a racist policy such as this one. Why does the Financial Aid Office do this? According to Cynthia Hartley, the Director of Financial Aid Planning and Policy Analysis at Stanford, it is "to make the Undergraduate experience richer for all." ....Full story in Opinion.....by Nels Hansen

WRC Campaign Hits the Mark
Last week I found myself lamenting to a friend of mine that the spectrum of people in this country who actually care about the lot of their brethren overseas seems about as slim as Callista Flockhart while fasting. It seems--I said in a rather irrational and emotional moment--that Americans tend to either xenophobic isolationism or patronizing, elitist, and anti-capitalist counterculturalism. Both groups seem to focus on what they dislike in the world: the former on those people and institutions outside their culture, and the latter on those within it. Neither does much to ameliorate the plight of the impoverished and oppressed who compose the majority of our species. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Alex Robbins
