Stanford Launches neighbors.stanford.edu
Stanford recently announced a new website, http://neighbors.stanford.edu, which will serve as a resource on Stanford to residents in nearby areas. The site, which is intended to improve the often stormy Stanford-community relations, will be a clearing house of information related to Stanford relevant to people not directly connected to Stanford.

The site includes information on Continuing Studies classes, development projects around campus (including the controversial General Use Permit), and information about how Stanford reduces its traffic impact on neighboring areas. It also includes information about Community Day, a day-long open house for area residents which will take place on April 7.

Hennessey Announces Living Wage
President Hennessey has announced that subcontracted workers at Stanford will be receive a "living wage," initially set at $10.10. The policy is similar to one adopted recently by San Jose.

The policy will apply to all workers on contracts lasting at least a year and with a value over $100,000. It will not include workers (either at Stanford or at contractors) covered by a collective bargaining agreement.

The Labor Justice Coalition, a group of students which campaigned for the policy, has criticized the decision, arguing that the wage is insufficient for a family with children to live on in the Palo Alto area. They have also denounced the fact that it does not include unionized workers as an effort at "union busting" by the University. The Coalition also accused President Hennessey of failing to keep a promise to consult them before making a decision.

Hennessey Denounced at ASSU Meeting
At the annual question and answer session between the ASSU and the University President, ASSU President Matt Brewer deferred the last question to members of the Labor Justice Coalition. The meeting degenerated into an open argument between the President and the Coalition members for several minutes before Undergraduate Senate Chair Alex Shvartsman regained control.

Roughly 35 members of the Coalition were present, all dressed in black.

The ASSU acknowledged that it was a breach of protocol to allow the members of the Labor Justice Coalition to ask the question, as well as allowing the question to be modified from the form that had been submitted prior. In addition, Coalition members have apologized for the fact that Provost Etchemendy, originally scheduled to hold a similar session two weeks after the President's, canceled his event the day after the incident.

Hoover Bells Return
The bells to Hoover Tower's carillon which were sent to the Netherlands to be recast two years ago, have returned to campus. The 48 bells will resume playing in March.

Latin American Studies Honor Program Renewed
Although the Committees of the Faculty Senate on Undergraduate and Graduate Studies have canceled the Bachelor and Master degree granting status of the Latin American Studies program, the Faculty Senate has renewed the Latin American Studies honors program.

The Latin American Studies program is expected to apply to reinstate the Bachelor and Master degree programs within a few years, after they have been retooled.
