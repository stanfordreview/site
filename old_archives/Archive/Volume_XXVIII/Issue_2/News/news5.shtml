Stephen Clausing Dies
Stephen Clausing, former lecturer in the Computer Science Department died on November 11, 2001, after a long battle with cancer. He was 49 years old.

Stephen taught at Stanford from 1993 through 1998. Like most lecturers, he was responsible for the first and second year programming courses, though he also taught the senior-year software engineering course, whichwas his specialty. As the primary instructor for CS105, Stephen revamped the curriculum to better suit the needs of a general education requirement.

Although most students took the course merely to satisfy a GRE, he achieved superior teacher evaluations combined with an above average workload. In fact, near the end of his stay at Stanford, CS105 had begun to rival the ever popular CS106 as the CS course to take. He himself always told students to take both, and many did, some going on to major in computer science, contrary to their own original intentions. In the software engineering course, he worked tirelessly to change the class from a "fun" projects course to a serious introduction to the subject. By his last year, the students gave him a 99% teacher evaluation rating combined with a 99% workload.

Stephen left Stanford for a position in project management at Apple Computer, where he remained employed until his death. He had a great love for Stanford and its students. He is survived by his wife and two children.
