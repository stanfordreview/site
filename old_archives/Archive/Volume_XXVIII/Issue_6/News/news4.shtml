Membership of Stanford Board of Trustees
by Harrison Osaki
Staff Writer

A close look at the Stanford Board of Trustees reveals a wide range of people in their professional lives. Many of the members have started their own businesses, such as Mark Oldman’s Vault, Inc., which purports to help employees "vault" into new jobs. Doris Fisher, a 1953 graduate and co-founder of The Gap clothing chain, is another Stanford professional whose work has gone into creating a new business. A few Board members are in public service. Cory Booker ran a widely known election in Newark for the Municipal Council. A few of the members are retired, most notably Peter S. Bing. Mr. Bing has been responsible for many donations made to Stanford, including Green Library’s Bing Wing. Other Board members have made significant contributions to Stanford, such as Winston Chen’s graduate scholarships. Victor Arias was responsible for the founding of the National Society of Hispanic MBA’s and has begun an organization dedicated to helping Latinos find jobs.

Stanford undergraduate alumni dominate the Board of Trustees; over two-thirds of the Board is a graduate of Stanford through the undergraduate program, and many other members attended the Law school or Business school. Some of the other connections that members have had to Stanford include the former Stanford Chief Financial Officer, the founder of the "Stanford in Washington" program, a member of the Stanford Humanities and Sciences Council, and the current president of Stanford.

The age range on the Board is fairly wide, but most of the members are still fairly young, as many of the members have graduated within the past two decades. Most of the Board members are from one of three geographic locations: California, New York and Texas.
