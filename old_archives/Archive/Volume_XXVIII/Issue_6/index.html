Membership of Stanford Board of Trustees
A close look at the Stanford Board of Trustees reveals a wide range of people in their professional lives. Many of the members have started their own businesses, such as Mark Oldman’s Vault, Inc., which purports to help employees "vault" into new jobs. Doris Fisher, a 1953 graduate and co-founder of The Gap clothing chain, is another Stanford professional whose work has gone into creating a new business. A few Board members are in public service. Cory Booker ran a widely known election in Newark for the Municipal Council. A few of the members are retired, most notably Peter S. Bing. Mr. Bing has been responsible for many donations made to Stanford, including Green Library’s Bing Wing. Other Board members have made significant contributions to Stanford, such as Winston Chen’s graduate scholarships. Victor Arias was responsible for the founding of the National Society of Hispanic MBA’s and has begun an organization dedicated to helping Latinos find jobs. ....Full story in News.....by Harrison Osaki

Movie Review: Hollywood Ending
Last Saturday, we carless freshmen made one of our rare ventures off campus to see Woody Allen's newest film, "Hollywood Ending." Did we enjoy the movie? Yes. Would we recommend it to someone looking for a laugh or a study break? Yes. Would we say it was a great film? No. "Hollywood Ending" is what we would call an "entertainment" film. It is a film that puts you in a good mood as you follow the predictable story lines and typecast characters...but not a film that warrants repeat viewings. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Ming Zhu and Hilary Connell

Restaurant Review: Left Bank Restaurant
After our theater excursion, we dined at Left Bank, a chiq French restaurant in Menlo Park with a bustling dining room and a full-service bar. The restaurant's social atmosphere provides an ideal location for meeting up with a group of friends for a nice evening out, but is not conducive to an intimate conversation (especially with the frequent cell phone rings reminding patrons they are smack-dab in the middle of suburbia). Service is not particularly fast, but certainly friendly. Reservations are strongly recommended for weekend dinners (although one can typically find a table quickly if he or she does not mind eating in the bar area). ....Full story in Opinion.....by Ming Zhu and Hilary Connell

Political Apathy Lets the Loudest Win
A large portion of Stanford's undergraduate student body proudly proclaims that they don't really care about politics, or the current state of the world. If a plane crashes, they didn't know anybody on it, and if a bill is passed, it probably won't change their lives very much. September 11th stirred a few of these people out of their complacency, as they realized that the world could potentially affect them very strongly, but, by now, most of them have returned to their apathetic state. This trend seems especially present in high schools and colleges around the nation, as intelligent young people put their energy into academic pursuits, sports, games, and others elements of our culture, while letting others worry about the sordid and byzantine realm of worldly affairs. This vast amount of apathy has left dorm email lists and political discussion to be dominated by the anti-business, anti-nuclear power, anti-capitalist environmentalists, with the occasional nativist, racist, far-right hate-monger popping up at other schools, and I for one am sick of this extremist rhetoric being passed off as the typical dichotomy of mainstream modern political thought. ....Full story in The Last Page.....by Joe Londsdale
