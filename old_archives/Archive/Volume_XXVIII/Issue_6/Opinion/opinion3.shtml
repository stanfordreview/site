Opinion
Restaurant Review: Left Bank Restaurant
by Ming Zhu and Hilary Connell
Staff Writers

Left Bank Restaurant
635 Santa Cruz Ave., Menlo Park
(650) 473-6543
After our theater excursion, we dined at Left Bank, a chiq French restaurant in Menlo Park with a bustling dining room and a full-service bar. The restaurant's social atmosphere provides an ideal location for meeting up with a group of friends for a nice evening out, but is not conducive to an intimate conversation (especially with the frequent cell phone rings reminding patrons they are smack-dab in the middle of suburbia). Service is not particularly fast, but certainly friendly. Reservations are strongly recommended for weekend dinners (although one can typically find a table quickly if he or she does not mind eating in the bar area).

We began our meal with the garlic-crusted shrimp; this appetizer--seven shrimp sauteed with garlic, lemon, and white wine--was everything it promised to be. All the flavors were there, not a single was missing. However, the lemon and white wine were definitely in greater proportion compared to the garlic. Of course, the relative lack of garlic could be considered tasteful or unpleasant, depending on personal preferences.

Ming: For the main dish, I chose the rotisserie chicken with boiled onion and garlic. The onion and garlic truly served no other purpose than to appeal to my eyes. My taste buds were politely asking for relief after a single bite of the mushy onion. The chicken was cooked thoroughly; it was juicy while still retaining the firmness of a roasted chicken breast. On the other hand, it exuded practically no taste whatsoever. Perhaps the chef spent so much time cooking the chicken that he forgot to season it afterward. I admit I am a great fan of salt and pepper, so my tastes may be overly heavy when it comes to poultry. However, I could still detect an utter lack of flavor. Hilary: I also ordered a poultry entrée--the duck l'orange, with a relish of apple wood-smoked bacon, celery, and onion on the side. I found my dish much more pleasing than Ming did hers. The meat was slightly tough, but it tasted wonderful. The highlight of my dinner, however, was unquestionably the sauce. The savory wine sauce with the perfect proportion of orange added a sharp yet sweet flavor to any food it dressed (the duck, the chicken, the side of mashed potatoes, etc.). I would strongly recommend this entrée, even to those who (like me) are not typically adventurous in their cuisine.

As for appearance of our food, Left Bank arranged both entrees invitingly. The sight, smell, and coloring of the dishes all received top scores in our book - it is merely in review of taste that we become more hesitant. But overall, Left Bank should be considered an upscale eatery that offers one the satisfaction of a nice dinner out at prices certainly not excessive when compared with other establishments in the Stanford vicinity. If comfortable French food is your thing, we heartily recommend you give Left Bank a try. If you are not a big fan of French, perhaps you should visit another restaurant.
