Smoke Signals
While many have been outraged over a UC Berkeley course which warned that "conservative thinkers are encouraged to seek other sections," the Chief chooses to look at the bright side: who doesn't want truth in advertising? Sure, it's a shame that conservatives aren't welcome, but at least they're warned. With that in mind, the Chief points out a few Stanford courses which might appreciate the same label:
Cultural and Social Anthropology 132 Science, Technology and Gender (Issues include "how the household became a specific site of consumerism and technology")
Drama 65 American Musical Theater: Broadway/Hollywood ("explores technology, ethnicity, gender...")
Drama 159W Dis/Ability Drama Dance and Film ("Disability joins race, class, gender, and sexuality as a representational category")
Linguistics 73 African American Vernacular English ("The history of expressive uses of African American English (in soundin' and rappin') and its educational imlications)
Comparative Literature 84Q Shakespeare, Playing, Gender ("topics: transvestism inside and outside of the theater, medical and other discussions of sex changes from female to male, hermaphrodites")
