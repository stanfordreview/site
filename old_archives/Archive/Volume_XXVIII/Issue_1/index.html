Charity and Social Entrepreneurship
Fliers on the Stanford campus tout every imaginable liberal cause. Checking out one of the omnipresent boards of fliers in my Toyon hallway yields a few daunting examples--"Promote Human Health in the Middle East [with taxpayers' dollars]"; "...Workers Deserve Better Pay [regardless of their free market worth]"; "Anti-Sweatshop Activists to Speak at Meyer [about how evil Nike is]." Next to these liberal postings is another flier with a liberal ring to it--Social Entrepreneurship: Creating Social Change in the 21st Century. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Joe Lonsdale

Ideological and Racial Discrimination at Stanford Disappointing
Discrimination. I knew the word. I knew that it existed. . . .but I had never experienced it before. I grew up in a little town on the west side of Michigan. It was a neighborhood of basketball hoops, Dodge minivans and White Anglo-Saxon Protestants. And yet, not once did I feel uncomfortable because of who I was. Unbelievably, I had to come to Stanford to discover what discrimination meant. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Ming Zhu
