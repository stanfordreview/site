Opinion
Interpersonal Dating is Oppressive and Unfair
by Nels Hansen
News Editor

In the past year I have attended and heard about various panels and groups discussing what they term an 'issue'; specifically, that of 'Inter-racial dating'. The essential difference between the ideology of these folks and that which was held by Bob Jones University is, first and foremost, the amount of melanin in their skin, but secondly the rationale behind the move.

Their message is simple: when white men establish relationships with minority women, the relationship is automatically exploitive and damaging. This is because of the history of oppression by white people to people of color, and the 'Power dynamic' that results, a sort of bad mojo if you will, relating to who holds the power in the relationship.

These are interesting concerns to have, but it seems to me as though their argument is not sufficiently fleshed out as of yet. Consider: how long has the oppression been going on? There is a book entitled "500 Years of Oppression," and I shall take that book at its word. But there is an even greater power dynamic going on than the one this group is acknowledging, and I am certain that this is simply negligence on their part, and that they will immediately switch their focus upon learning of it. I am talking about women.

The oppression of women by men is nearly universal; it has taken place in nearly every world culture for an extensive period of time. Some say the father of patriarchy must have been someone around the time of the Epic of Gilgamesh, a tale of 2 guys thumbing their nose at the matriarchal power structure. This was about 5000 years ago, and it is a fairly conservative estimate as to the length of history of the oppression of women.

Also, the power dynamic in an Intergender relationship has historically been skewed dramatically to the side of the Male, and as we know these historical trends dictate, just as they do in Interracial dating, that the sins of the group carry on down to the individual (a bit like the rationale used by wise elementary school teachers: "Joey ruined it for everyone").

As such, it seems obvious that we must immediately form panels 'discussing' (by which I mean attacking) any and all Intergender relationships. Ban Intergender dating! But why stop there? This oppression is certainly not a quantitative item, and probably can never be quantified. The power dynamic part of the equation suffers the same difficulty. So doesn't it follow that any group which has a history wavering slightly to one side in both oppression and power dynamic should not interact?

We must therefore immediately work to ban inter-handed dating (people of unusual handedness, as we lefties prefer to be called, have long been oppressed by those ordinary-handed people). Even today in the land of China, many left-handed children are, in accordance with tradition, forced to use their right hand in conformity with some barbaric tradition! Also, nearly everything set up to be 'handed' is in many countries exclusively set up for right-handed people. I understand that in some countries, the left hand is relegated to a bottom-wiping function! The history of oppression and the sporadic power-dynamics of Ôhandedness' unquestionably rival that of at least one or two inter-ethnic conflicts. However, it is probably valid to say that the history of white-black race relations is even more marred; here, at least one man was actually compelled to cross the line and switch affiliations (did you know that Michael Jackson was not born looking like that?).

Next on my list is inter-socioeconomic class dating. This is an easy one. The rich have long exploited the poor for their own gain, paying the poor to work in factories or in any other of a wide variety of oppressive relationships. Also, in these relationships, it seems fair to say that the rich person is probably always 'the boss' of the relationship. Therefore, these relationships too are terrible and destructive.

Now how about inter-generational dating? Elderly people have long been 'respected' and 'venerated' more than their younger, sometimes even more talented, counterparts merely based on the fact that these other people happen to be elderly. Also, typically the leader of a human community is older than most of its constituents [no doubt because of age discrimination]. The tradition of 'respect for one's elders' means that a 1 nanosecond difference in age is enough to invoke ageism, and so anyone not born at the precise instant that another was is unsuitable for dating.

And as for inter-intellectual ability dating, that's right out. Historically, the better tacticians, carpenters, etc., have been respected far more than those perceived by society to be of lesser talent, and many times, when a struggle breaks out, it is 'brains over brawn'. This terrible cycle seems to have no end, as today this is perhaps truer than at any other time in history. It is clear to me that an IQ point of difference will transform any relationship into one where the 'smart person' exploits the other, probably for sex.

[editor's note -- further research into this particular point has uncovered that the unfounded assumption that "talent" or "intellect" can be in any way quantitatively compared is naive and bigoted. Realistically, people are seen as "smart" by our prejudiced society when they try to act "white." Please excuse our confused colleague, he did not mean to sound racist.]

These inherent points of unfairness are no doubt the source of all our social ills, and are also the reason why many Stanford students cannot find a decent date and are deeply dissatisfied with their pathetic lives, and the social atmosphere in which they live. Now, what sort of person should we be looking for? Ideally, someone who is of the same gender, the same handedness, the same exact age, the same intellectual ability, and the same socioeconomic class. I have a hunch about who that is.

In short, we must restrict our amorous interactions exclusively to ourselves. This is the natural extension of the logic of those laudable inter-racial dating 'panels' (read: whiner support groups). Oh, and having a relationship with God? Are you kidding? Talk about a bad power-dynamic.

Nels will now go sit in his room and try to avoid oppressing himself
