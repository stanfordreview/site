Axess Axed
The Office of Academic Computing has acknowledged that the new Axess system, responsible for tracking all grades, courses, and most other data for all Stanford students, will be taken off line at the end of the year. The system has required dramatic policy changes throughout the University, including an end to students taking more than twenty units, and end to advisor codes, and a number of other policies. Administrators noted that the computer system actually got more sleep than more students, something not normally required of software programs.

In place of Axess, Stanford will return to using hand-processed sheets filled in with no-2 pencils. The Office of Academic Computing has noted that the system will be faster than the old one, since there will no extended waits for pages to load.

Stanford Announces Solar Power Plan
Acknowledging that he was "unable to refuse" the demands of the ASSU Undergraduate Senate, President Hennessey has pledged to have 50% of campus power provided by solar enery by 2010. He noted that the primary motivation was the strong support from the ASSU, which passed a resolution last quarter and has taken no further action on the subject.

Hoover Institution Ejected From Campus
A narrowly divided vote of the Board of Trustees gave the Hoover Institution one week to remove all its belongings from campus. It initially appeared that the Institution would be able to remove all its possessions in that time, forfeiting them to the University. Hoover Director John Raisian held an emergency telethon, including guest appearances by an assortment of conservative icons such as Nobel prize winning economist Milton Friedman and long-time Republican advisor Martin Anderson. A series of last minute donations from Enron executives resulted in enough money to lift Hoover Tower from its foundations with helicopters and fly it to its new location in Orange County. It also resulted in a series of intellectual articles from Hoover Fellows blaming the Clinton administration for Enron's troubles.

Stanford plans to use the freed space to build the world's tallest parking garage, planned to have the same dimensions as the tower it replaces.

Stanford Adopts Prevailing Wage
President Hennessey has acceded to the demands of the Stanford Labor Action Committee, announcing at a recent Faculty Senate meeting a broad prevailing wage policy, under which all University employees will be guaranteed the same wage. The wage will be the same regardless of unionization or job details, although it will allow increases for seniority.

The Faculty Senate had a mixed reaction to the announcement, with some professors lauding it as a firm step towards fair labor practices, while others expressed concern that paying faculty $13.50 an hour will not be competitive with other top Universities.

SLAC has denounced the decision, objecting that the new policy was made without worker input, and that the policy does not provide the "normative wage" that they have demanded all along.
