Activists Right: Government Thugs Crack Down on Dissent
by Alex Robbins
Comrade

Late last night federal forces made several simultaneous assaults against politically suspect organizations throughout the Stanford Campus.

At approximately 2:45 AM, as most slept soundly nestled in their beds and the occassional inebriated reveler meandered down the streets, three black, unmarked helicopters hovered over the Stanford Columbae house. Paramilitary federal agents descended nylon ropes and smashed through the windows of slumbering student activists.

Official sources report that the raid was a response to the post-September 11 rash of self-righteous and unthinkingly paranoiac bombast promulgated by the Stanford elite.

According to deputy U.S. marshal Jack Ramsey, "We in power simply couldn't stand it anymore. For years we've tried to act constitutionally; we've tried to respect civil rights; we've tried to promote justice and act with honor." Pausing for a moment, a nascent tear forming just below his left eye, Mr. Ramsey continued. "We realized that regardless of how we behaved, or what we did, these people would still denounce us as imperialist thugs. That's just not right, you know, it's really not right. We have feelings too. We just couldn't deal with it anymore."

He then looked over and shot a tearful smile to a fellow officer, Gus Smith. "So we just said 'hell with it, let's kick some snot-nose ass, hey, Gus?"

Mr. Smith, smiling as he drove his boot into the throat of the next long-haired, unwashed, self-proclaimed revolutionary, ceased pressing the barrel of his gun into the young man's eye long enough to turn and respond with a grin: "What the hell! If people are gonna call me a fascist thug, I might as well get some fun out of it!"

Similar events unfolded across campus. Eric Roberts, professor of computer science, was handcuffed to a golf cart and forced to run behind it for two hours, on the charge of being a "dirty, washed-up hippie." Former students and collegues watched on helplessly and for the most part sympathetically, except for one bitter old college who kept screaming "Enchanter, eh?? Enchant your way out of this, will ya?!"

Officers also performed brutal and warrantless late-night raids on Synergy, the Women's Center, the Comparative Studies in Race and Ethnicity department, Narnia, the Enchanted Broccoli Forest, and the OSA and President's Office for good measure. Casa Zapata had been targeted but was left unmolested at the last minute, after officers viewing the murals busted out laughing at one scene depicting a revolutionary fighter trying to use flowers as a firearm as he crouched behind a box riddled with bullet holes. "Stupid revolutionaries," remarked one officer wistfully, turning to his cohorts. "Let's just go trash Columbae again."

In a bizarre addition, the Coho and the Texaco station were both decimated after a number of officers were rudely refused service and expelled from the premises when the employees working there tried to close hours before the appropriate, posted times.

One Hoover Fellow witnessed one of these scenes and tried to protest, shouting declamations about governmental oppression and totalitarian police tactics. He was treated carefully by officers, who knew he was a Republican and therefore might be armed, but one captive activist lifted his head out of the fountain long enough to shout, "Take your racist, classist, imperialist, genderist, aptitudist, and perverted ideas of freedom away from me! I want no part of them! You're sick! Some of us actually want government to make the world a better, safer, kinder place!"

Reactions have not been entirely negative, however. Middle-class moderates across the country are jubilant that the privileged yet revolutionary elites at Stanford have finally "gotten theirs for whining." One parent remarked, "I sent my kid to school so she could get a damn job, not become a Zapatista. Now she won't stop speaking Spanish and bitching about 'justicia' when I try to get her to do the dishes or fold the laundry. Damn feminists."

Even some of the victims are overjoyed. One victim smiled from the inside of her two-by-two-foot cell. "This proves what we've been saying all along! The imperialists have shown themselves! At last, at last, I can be a martyr for my cause without having to ask my Daddy for a plane ticket to a third world country!"
