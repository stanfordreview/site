Point: Be an "Upper," not a "Downer"
The main problem with my previously conservative beliefs, I've realized, is the fact that I was always so negative about everything. For example, someone would be trying to help people by, say, raising the living wage to a reasonable level. They were trying to do something good for society with what they'd thought up or heard from others. That's what I call being an "upper". I, on the other hand, would see this idea and start explaining why I thought it didn't work. Of course, economics and reality is much too complicated to truly know what's going to happen--by exploring these negative opinions, I was being a "downer." I was taking an idea that was meant to help people, and attacking it. What we need to do instead is to take these ideas and implement them, and then bask in their warm and happy glow. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Joe Lonsdale

Counterpoint: The Essence of Moral Reason
The key to moral reason is to start with your presumptions and look for the most defensible way to support them. Only in this way can your true self--your first impressions--remain untainted by all the misleading influences that abound in the world. For instance, as a generous human being, I naturally love all animals, and just as I wouldn't eat a grandparent who I love, neither would I eat an animal. "I love you cattle" I wave out the window. ....Full story in Opinion.....by Alex Rawls
