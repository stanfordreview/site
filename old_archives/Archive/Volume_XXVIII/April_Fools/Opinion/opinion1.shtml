Opinion
Point: Be an "Upper," not a "Downer"
by Joe Lonsdale
Comrade

The main problem with my previously conservative beliefs, I've realized, is the fact that I was always so negative about everything. For example, someone would be trying to help people by, say, raising the living wage to a reasonable level. They were trying to do something good for society with what they'd thought up or heard from others. That's what I call being an "upper". I, on the other hand, would see this idea and start explaining why I thought it didn't work. Of course, economics and reality is much too complicated to truly know what's going to happen--by exploring these negative opinions, I was being a "downer." I was taking an idea that was meant to help people, and attacking it. What we need to do instead is to take these ideas and implement them, and then bask in their warm and happy glow.

I've come to realize that downers are causing a large part of society's major problems. Just think of all the wonderful, good-feeling ideas that exist out there that downers are working to destroy. Helping the environment, for instance--how can anyone but a downer be against any aspect of something this positive for our society, and for our children's society, and our children's children's society, and for their children too, if nukes don't kill them first? Unless you truly enjoy telling other people that their ideas are wrong--something good students learn never to do in today's high schools--then why would you disagree with those of us working to save mother nature?

Just the other day, I was enjoying my newfound beliefs by explaining to the food truck guy that paper decomposes, but Styrofoam doesn't--using paper helps mother nature, and he should boycott people who try to destroy nature by selling him Styrofoam. Suddenly, a "downer" approached--a man from the nearby Hoover Institution. He saw my smiling face, and immediately jumped into his downer routine--he started explaining that Styrofoam is made from a naturally occurring substance that doesn't decompose anyway. "In effect, by using Styrofoam, you're just moving styrene around the Earth's crust, and getting a great benefit from it in the meantime," he almost vehemently said, with a probably false smile.

Pshhh!! I replied with the only retort this downer deserved--"You're an evil person," I explained, before carefully riding off with my hot coffee, the burning through the sides of the paper cup reminding me of my high-minded, happy ideas. I don't know about you, but not filling up landfills and not ruining the world for my children is more important to me than not burning my hands when I drink hot beverages. What a selfish man, that Hoover guy.

Some of you may not agree with my new ideas--this especially includes those of you who are conservative adults, and have had the downer instinct long pounded into your simple, cruel, and naive minds. Let me clear of you of your misconceptions by explaining why it's important that the government pays for everyone's healthcare.

Stop! I know what you downers are thinking. You're thinking negative thoughts. You have probably been brainwashed by people like the crazy Ayn Rand, who thinks that freedom entails doing what you want with your own money. That's not freedom--that's being mean and not helping people. You are probably also thinking--"that idea is expensive". Well, let's look at the proper way to respond to this healthcare plan.

Uppers hear this truly happy, helpful idea, and they say, "That's a great idea! Everyone would have healthcare!" See--they focus on the positives. Think of how the oppressed people in our society, who have so many disadvantages, can never do as well as the majority. Think of how wonderful it would be to provide for their children. Do you NOT want to provide for children's health? Come to think of it--I don't even remember how I was ever against these healthcare ideas. I guess, like other downers, I never stopped to think of all the people we could help.

Yes--helping people. That's what we need to focus on. After all, why are we here, if not to love and help each other? By being a downer, you are focusing on aspects that help nobody, and are best left alone. Remember--the next time you hear an idea that sounds like it will help a minority, a child, a woman, a poor person, or mother nature--it's a good idea. By being an upper, we can all work together towards a society where everyone helps everyone else under law, and nobody wastes time seeking personal profit or focuses on themselves and their downer opinions. Just remember--anything that has to do with the unimportant details of "reality" is not something we need to concern ourselves with. Only by blindly helping everyone around us who is not an advantaged Caucasian or Asian person, will we create a better, brighter, fairer, and happier future.
